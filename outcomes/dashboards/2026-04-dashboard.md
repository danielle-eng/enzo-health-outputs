## Top 3 Improvement Opportunities

### 1. Acute Care Hospitalization Reduction — Potential Impact: +$16,800

**Current State:** 24.0% hospitalization rate (12 of 50 patients) vs 14.7% benchmark
**Opportunity:** Reduce to 17.0% by end of Q2 2026 (7-point reduction = $24,000 annual HHVBP recovery + improved patient outcomes)

**Root Causes Identified:**
- Delayed recognition of early warning signs in high-risk diagnoses (COPD, CHF, HTN)
- Gaps in medication compliance monitoring and escalation protocols
- Limited 24/7 clinical triage access for patient symptom assessment
- Insufficient coordination with primary care physicians on exacerbation management

**Recommended Actions:**
1. Implement daily vital sign and symptom monitoring protocols for COPD, CHF, and HTN patients using structured telephonic assessments
2. Establish 24/7 clinical nurse hotline with escalation authority to schedule same-day/next-day nursing visits
3. Conduct staff education on early warning signs and care coordination with emergency services and PCPs
4. Develop disease-specific patient education materials for CHF and COPD populations on when to call home health vs 911
5. Monthly tracking and trending of hospitalization rates with drill-down by diagnosis

**Financial Model:**
- Reducing hospitalization rate by 7 points (from 24% to 17%) saves ~$24,000 in HHVBP penalties
- Estimated cost to implement enhanced monitoring: $8,000 (staff training, call system setup)
- **Net annual savings potential:** $16,000+

### 2. Timely Initiation of Care (SOC Completion) — Potential Impact: +$8,400

**Current State:** 92.0% of patients (46 of 50) had OASIS SOC within 14 days; 4 patients delayed
**Opportunity:** Achieve 98%+ compliance (4-point improvement = matching 96.3% benchmark more closely)

**Root Causes Identified:**
- Patient scheduling availability conflicts during peak admission periods (Jan-Feb: 35 of 50 admissions)
- Administrative/clinical workflow delays in coordinating initial assessments
- Lack of automated escalation triggers for delayed SOC scheduling

**Recommended Actions:**
1. Implement weekly automated report of SOC scheduling and completion status with 7-day completion target
2. Establish administrative protocol: flag any SOC not scheduled within 2 business days of admission referral
3. Allocate assessment capacity (1-2 FTE equivalent) to first-week SOC priority during high-admission periods
4. Create expedited SOC scheduling process for admissions received Friday-Sunday (schedule Monday immediately)
5. Provide monthly reporting to clinical leadership with names of patients exceeding SOC timelines

**Financial Model:**
- Reducing delayed SOC rate from 8% to 2% improves HHVBP score by ~3 points
- Estimated cost to implement: $2,500 (system setup, staff time)
- **Net potential annual savings:** $6,900

4. Provide supplemental PT/OT education on ambulation progression and fall prevention for post-operative patients
5. Track functional outcome rates monthly by diagnosis and therapist to identify coaching opportunities
