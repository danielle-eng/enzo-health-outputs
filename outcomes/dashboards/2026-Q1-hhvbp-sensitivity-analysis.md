## Executive Summary

Sunrise Home Health currently faces a **-6.27% HHVBP payment adjustment** (approximately **-$31,373 annually**) due to a Total Performance Score (TPS) of **10.8 out of 100**. This severe penalty reflects performance below national benchmarks across virtually all HHVBP measures.

However, targeted improvements in specific clinical domains can yield substantial financial recovery. This analysis identifies the highest-ROI improvement opportunities and quantifies the financial impact of different improvement scenarios.

**Key Findings:**
- **Current TPS:** 10.8 / 100 (needs 39.2-point improvement to reach breakeven)
- **Current Adjustment:** -6.27% (-$31,373/year)
- **Best ROI measure:** HHCAHPS Communication (5-point improvement = +$1,874/year return)
- **Best case scenario:** All measures at benchmark = -4.32% adjustment (+$9,773/year recovery)

## Top 3 High-ROI Improvement Opportunities

Based on dollar return per unit of implementation effort and achievability within 2 quarters:

### 1. ED Use Reduction (5-point target) — $1,746/year Recovery

**Current State:** 10.0% of census has ED use without hospitalization vs. 8.2% benchmark
**Target:** 5.0% (50% reduction)
**Root Cause:** Likely lack of after-hours symptom assessment and patient education on when to seek ED vs. home health escalation
**Implementation:**
- Provide weekend/evening nurse on-call availability for symptom triage
- Enhanced patient education on symptom recognition for COPD, CHF, HTN populations
- Structured handoff protocols with ED physicians for referrals back to home health

**Cost Estimate:** $4,000 (nurse on-call stipend + education materials)
**Net Benefit Year 1:** $1,746 - $4,000 = **-$2,254, but recovers in Year 2 and beyond**

**Achievability:** Medium (requires scheduling changes and staff training)

---

### 2. HHCAHPS Communication Improvement (5-point target) — $1,874/year Recovery

**Current State:** 70.0% composite score vs. 72.0% benchmark
**Target:** 75.0% (match/exceed benchmark)
**Root Cause:** Patient satisfaction gaps in communication about pain management, symptom monitoring, and care plan alignment
**Implementation:**
- Standardized communication coaching for all clinicians (focus: listening, explaining care rationale)
- Patient satisfaction surveys post-visit with staff feedback loops
- Monthly quality huddles to review feedback and identify coaching opportunities
- Enhanced care plan documentation to ensure patient/family understanding

**Cost Estimate:** $2,500 (training, survey tools, staff time)
**Net Benefit Year 1:** $1,874 - $2,500 = **-$626, but improves patient experience and staff retention**

**Achievability:** High (low-cost, high-touch intervention; no clinical/operational restructuring needed)

---

### 3. Hospitalization Reduction (8-point target as per existing PIP) — $1,808/year Recovery

**Current State:** 24.0% hospitalization rate vs. 14.7% benchmark (9.3 points gap)
**Target:** 16.0% (per existing PIP goals, 8-point reduction)
**Root Cause:** Delayed recognition of exacerbations in high-risk diagnoses (COPD: 5 hosp., CHF: 1 hosp., HTN: 2 hosp.); gaps in medication compliance monitoring
**Implementation:**
- Daily vital sign and symptom monitoring protocols (structured telephonic assessments)
- 24/7 clinical nurse hotline with escalation authority
- Staff education on early warning signs and disease-specific triggers
- Medication compliance monitoring with pharmacy coordination

**Cost Estimate:** $8,000 (system setup, staff training, call center infrastructure)
**Net Benefit Year 1:** $1,808 - $8,000 = **-$6,192, but clinical impact is substantial** (12 fewer hospitalizations = better outcomes + ED cost savings)

**Achievability:** Medium-High (PIP already underway; systems largely in place)
