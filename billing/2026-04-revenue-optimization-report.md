## LUPA Risk Assessment Across Episodes

**What is LUPA?** Low Utilization Payment Adjustment. When a 60-day episode contains fewer visits than a threshold amount, Medicare pays a per-visit rate instead of the 30-day bundled amount. This significantly reduces reimbursement.

### PT001 (Wounds Group)
- **LUPA Threshold:** 4 visits per 30-day period
- **Current Visit Status:** 4 visits to date (as of April 4)
- **Projected Total Visits:** 8.6 over 60-day episode
- **Days Remaining in 30-Day Review Period:** 16
- **Risk Assessment:** LOW
- **Explanation:** Already at threshold with 16 days remaining. Strong visit pattern emerging. Per-visit cost for wound care is high, so LUPA is not a threat.

### PT002 (Cardiac/MMTA)
- **LUPA Threshold:** 6 visits per 30-day period
- **Current Visit Status:** 8 visits to date (already exceeded threshold)
- **Projected Total Visits:** 17.1 over 60-day episode
- **Days Remaining in 30-Day Review Period:** 16
- **Risk Assessment:** VERY LOW
- **Explanation:** Exceeds LUPA threshold by 33%. Cardiac patients with comorbidities typically require frequent monitoring and education, supporting current utilization pattern.

### PT003 (General/MMTA Orthopedic)
- **LUPA Threshold:** 4 visits per 30-day period
- **Current Visit Status:** 2 visits to date (as of April 4)
- **Projected Total Visits:** 15.0 over 60-day episode
- **Days Remaining in 30-Day Review Period:** 26
- **Risk Assessment:** LOW
- **Explanation:** Currently below threshold at Day 16 of 60-day episode, but projection shows strong upward trajectory. Post-surgical orthopedic cases typically require 2-3 weeks before intensive physical therapy begins. With 26 days remaining, patient should easily reach 4-visit threshold by end of 30-day period.

**Monitoring Recommendation for PT003:** Track week of April 15-21 to ensure physical therapy visits are initiated on schedule. If PT is delayed beyond Day 30 of episode, LUPA risk would increase.
