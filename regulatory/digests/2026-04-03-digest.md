## 🔴 Urgent — Action Required

### OASIS-E2 Implementation - Effective April 1, 2026
**Source:** [CMS OASIS Data Sets](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/quality/home-health/oasis-data-sets)
**Date Issued:** March 2026
**Effective Date:** April 1, 2026
**Summary:** The OASIS-E2 data collection instrument is now mandatory for all home health patients effective April 1, 2026. All assessments with a completion date on or after April 1 must use OASIS-E2, including significant item changes and the removal of COVID-19 vaccination items.

**Impact on Home Health Agencies:** Home health agencies must immediately implement OASIS-E2 in all clinical workflows. Key changes include replacement of M0069 (Gender) with A0810 (Sex), removal of O0350 (COVID-19 vaccination status), addition of sensory assessment items (Hearing, Vision, Language) to Resumption of Care encounters, and modification of A1250 (transportation). All clinical staff must be trained on new data elements and response options. As of July 1, 2025, OASIS data collection applies to ALL patients regardless of payer source (not just Medicare), expanding documentation burden.

**Enzo Product Implications:**
- **Scribe:** Must update OASIS item references to reflect E2 changes; remove COVID-19 vaccination item from templates; add prompts for sensory assessment items; ensure gender/sex field mapping is correct. Requires immediate testing and deployment.
- **Intake:** Update initial assessment forms to capture all OASIS-E2 required items; ensure data validation rules reflect new item requirements and response options.
- **Scheduling:** May need to flag assessments due on or after April 1 to ensure E2 version is used; coordinate with Scribe to ensure proper data element usage.
- **QAPI:** Monitor for any documentation errors related to OASIS-E2 transition; establish audit protocols for correct item completion; track compliance with April 1 transition deadline.

**Action Required By:** April 1, 2026

### CY 2026 Home Health PPS Final Rule - Effective January 1, 2026 (Already in Effect)
**Source:** [CMS CY 2026 Home Health Prospective Payment System Final Rule](https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/fact-sheets/calendar-year-cy-2026-home-health-prospective-payment-system-final-rule-cms-1828-f)
**Date Issued:** November 28, 2025 (Federal Register December 2, 2025)
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Summary:** CMS finalized the 2026 Home Health PPS rate update with a net 1.3% aggregate payment reduction ($220 million). The rule includes removal of four OASIS assessment items, elimination of the COVID-19 vaccine quality measure, new functional assessment measures, and updates to case-mix weights, comorbidity subgroups, and LUPA thresholds.

**Impact on Home Health Agencies:** Home health agencies are experiencing a 1.3% aggregate payment decrease effective January 1, 2026, representing approximately $19 per 30-day episode reduction on average. Agencies must remove four assessment items from documentation: one Living Situation item, two Food items, and one Utilities item. The removal of the COVID-19 vaccine measure from quality reporting reduces documentation burden for that specific measure. Agencies must implement new quality measures related to improvement in bathing, upper body dressing, and lower body dressing (OASIS-based), plus the Medicare Spending Per Beneficiary for Post-Acute Care claims-based measure. Case-mix weights have been recalibrated using 2024 PDGM behavior data, affecting payment calculations for all episodes.

**Enzo Product Implications:**
- **Scribe:** Remove COVID-19 vaccine assessment item from OASIS templates; remove four deprecated assessment items (living situation, food, utilities); implement logic to capture new bathing and dressing functional status items. Update clinical documentation workflows to ensure proper functional assessment documentation.
- **Intake:** Update initial assessment forms to remove deprecated items; ensure new functional measures are documented at appropriate assessment timepoints; update data validation to ensure new measures are present and complete.
- **Scheduling:** No direct scheduling impact, but may need to alert agencies to ensure all episodes are assigned correct PDGM groups based on updated case-mix weights.
- **QAPI:** Update quality measure calculations to reflect removal of COVID-19 measure; add new bathing/dressing outcome measures and MSPB-PAC measure to performance tracking; monitor payment adequacy relative to 1.3% reduction; adjust benchmarks for new measure set.

**Action Required By:** Ongoing monitoring; new measures must be calculated for CY 2026 quality reporting

### Home Health Value-Based Purchasing (HHVBP) Model - CY 2026 Updates
**Source:** [CMS Expanded Home Health Value-Based Purchasing Model](https://www.cms.gov/priorities/innovation/innovation-models/expanded-home-health-value-based-purchasing-model)
**Date Issued:** December 2025 (Final Rule)
**Summary:** CY 2026 is the fourth performance year of the expanded HHVBP Model. CMS is removing three of five HHCAHPS measures (due to broader HHCAHPS updates) and adding four new measures (one claims-based MSPB-PAC measure and three OASIS-based bathing/dressing measures). The OASIS portion now accounts for 40% of Total Performance Score.

**Why It Matters:** Home health agencies participating in HHVBP will see significant changes to their performance scoring methodology. Removal of HHCAHPS measures eliminates 18% of the Total Performance Score, requiring recalibration of all component weights. The addition of new bathing and dressing measures increases focus on functional outcomes. Performance in CY 2026 will determine payment adjustments for CY 2028, creating a 2-year lag. Agencies must ensure proper documentation of the new functional measures to accurately reflect their performance.

**Enzo Product Implications:**
- **Scribe:** Ensure bathing and dressing functional status items (OASIS M1810, M1820, M1830 or equivalent E2 items) are captured with appropriate frequency and detail; monitor compliance with new measure documentation requirements.
- **Intake:** Verify that initial and comprehensive assessments capture all required functional status items for HHVBP measure calculation.
- **Scheduling:** No direct impact, but monitor for any changes to assessment scheduling requirements related to new functional measures.
- **QAPI:** Update HHVBP performance calculations to reflect new measure set; remove three HHCAHPS measures from scoring; add new bathing/dressing and MSPB-PAC measures; recalibrate performance targets based on new scoring weights; monitor performance trajectories against new benchmarks.

### OIG Work Plan - Home Health Compliance Audits
**Source:** [HHS OIG Work Plan - Home Health Compliance with Medicare Requirements](https://oig.hhs.gov/reports/work-plan/browse-work-plan-projects/w-00-24-35712/)
**Date Issued:** Ongoing (FY 2026)
**Summary:** OIG continues active audits of home health agencies for Medicare compliance, with multiple completed audits in February 2026. Historical improper payment error rates for home health claims remain high (51.4% in 2014, approximately $9.4 billion). OIG is working with state partners to identify and reduce improper payments in HHA, hospice, and DME services.

**Why It Matters:** Home health agencies face ongoing scrutiny from OIG audits focused on improper payments, documentation accuracy, and compliance with Medicare coverage requirements. The focus on Federal-State partnerships may increase audit frequency and scope in participating states. Agencies should expect audits to examine clinical documentation, assessment accuracy, compliance with face-to-face requirements, and coding/billing practices. Non-compliance findings can result in substantial recoupments, penalties, and exclusion from Medicare.

**Enzo Product Implications:**
- **Scribe:** Ensure comprehensive and accurate clinical documentation that supports medical necessity and justifies skilled care requirements; implement audit-ready documentation standards.
- **Intake:** Maintain accurate assessment documentation; ensure all required clinical information is captured to support care plan development and medical necessity.
- **Scheduling:** Monitor for any patterns of documentation gaps or compliance issues that might trigger OIG scrutiny.
- **QAPI:** Implement proactive compliance monitoring and audit readiness protocols; establish documentation accuracy tracking; develop internal audit procedures aligned with OIG focus areas (medical necessity, face-to-face, coverage requirements, coding accuracy).
