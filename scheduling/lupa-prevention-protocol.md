# LUPA Prevention Protocol
## Step-by-Step Guide for Scheduling Coordinators

**Enzo Health — SUNRISE Agency**
**Effective Date:** April 4, 2026
**Audience:** Scheduling Coordinators, Intake Specialists, Scheduling Supervisors

---

## WHAT IS LUPA?

**LUPA** = **Low Utilization Payment Adjustment**

When a home health patient receives **fewer than the minimum required visits** in a 30-day episode, Medicare reduces the episode payment by approximately 12–20% (about $500–800 per episode).

**Why It Matters:**
- Every LUPA incident reduces agency revenue
- LUPA patterns can trigger Medicare audits
- LUPA impacts clinician productivity metrics
- Preventable LUPAs damage agency reputation with payers

**Your Role:** You prevent LUPA by ensuring patients receive ordered visits **on schedule**.

---

## QUICK START: 5-MINUTE RESPONSE

When the scheduling system flags a patient as **LUPA Risk** (usually marked RED or YELLOW in weekly compliance report):

1. **Read the Flag** (takes 30 seconds)
   - Patient name, ID, PDGM group
   - Discipline (PT, SLP, SN, OT, etc.) that is short visits
   - Visits needed: current visit count vs. required minimum
   - Days remaining in current 30-day cycle

2. **Assess Feasibility** (takes 2 minutes)
   - Can the patient be seen THIS WEEK? Call patient/caregiver (or check recent notes)
   - Is the patient willing and available?
   - Is the required clinician available?

3. **Take Action** (takes 1–2 minutes)
   - **IF YES:** Schedule the visit(s) immediately; mark as "LUPA prevention visit"
   - **IF NO:** Follow escalation path (see below)

## STEP-BY-STEP WORKFLOW

### STEP 1: Identify LUPA Risk (You receive this notification)

**Weekly Report:** Every Friday afternoon, you receive the Scheduling & Visit Frequency Compliance Report.

**Scan for LUPA Risk Patients:**
- Look for patients with compliance < the LUPA threshold for their group (see table below)
- Example flag: "Michael Brown (PT005): PT 9/17 visits (below 12 expected); 5 days remaining"

**LUPA Thresholds by PDGM Clinical Group:**

| PDGM Clinical Group | Min Visits/30 Days | LUPA Trigger |
|--------------------|--------------------|--------------|
| MMTA-Cardiac/Circulatory | 3 | <3 visits |
| MMTA-Endocrine | 3 | <3 visits |
| MMTA-GI/GU | 3 | <3 visits |
| MMTA-Infectious Disease | 3 | <3 visits |
| MMTA-Neuro | 3 | <3 visits |
| MMTA-Respiratory | 3 | <3 visits |
| MMTA-Other | 3 | <3 visits |
| Behavioral Health | 3 | <3 visits |
| Complex Nursing Interventions | 4 | <4 visits |
| MS Rehab | 3 | <3 visits |
| Neuro Rehab | 3 | <3 visits |
| Ortho Rehab | 3 | <3 visits |

**Most patients need minimum 3 visits/30 days. Complex Nursing patients need 4.**

---

### STEP 2: Contact Patient to Assess Availability

**Goal:** Determine if visit can be scheduled in remaining days of 30-day cycle

**Make a Brief Phone Call or Check EMR Notes:**

*Script (if calling patient/caregiver):*

> "Hi [Patient Name], this is [Your Name] from Enzo Health scheduling. Dr. [clinician] has ordered [discipline] visits to help with your [condition]. We want to make sure you're getting all your visits—are you available for a visit this [day of week]? Would morning or afternoon work better?"

**Document (in EMR or note):**
- Date/time of contact attempt
- Whether patient answered and what they said
- If unavailable: reason (traveling, no transportation, fatigue, etc.)
- If available: preferred date/time for visit

**Typical Response Time:** 1–2 hours (call during business hours)

---

### STEP 3: Schedule the Visit (If Patient Is Available)

**Assign to Clinician:**
- **First choice:** Primary clinician for that discipline
- **Second choice:** Any available clinician in that discipline (backfill)
- **Telehealth option:** For therapy (PT, OT, SLP) if in-person unavailable—check physician POC to confirm telehealth is allowed

**Mark Visit in Schedule:**
- **Add note:** "LUPA prevention visit — required to meet episode minimum"
- **Priority:** Flag as high-priority to prevent cancellation/reschedule

**Confirm with Clinician:**
- Brief message: "PT/SLP/OT visit needed by [date] for [patient]. Clinically appropriate per POC; required to prevent LUPA. Can you accommodate?"

**Timeline:**
- Ideally schedule within 24 hours of notification
- Must schedule before end of current 30-day cycle (5–7 days remaining typically)

---

### STEP 4: If Visit Cannot Be Scheduled → Escalation

**Follow this escalation path based on reason:**

#### **Scenario A: Patient Is Unavailable or Declines Visit**
- *Reason:* "Patient traveling," "No transportation," "Patient refuses," "Insufficient pain level"

**Action:**
1. Document patient declination in EMR with specific reason
2. **Escalate to Clinical Supervisor (same day):**
   - Copy Clinical Supervisor on email with documentation
   - Include: Patient name, visit type, reason for unavailability, dates remaining
   - Subject: "LUPA Risk: [Patient Name] — [Discipline] Visit Cannot Be Scheduled"

**Clinical Supervisor Will:**
- Assess if visit is clinically necessary despite patient reluctance
- Contact ordering physician if needed to discuss frequency adjustment
- Make final call: force the visit (in-person) vs. allow discharge vs. accept LUPA risk

**Document Outcome:**
- Clinical Supervisor decision in EMR
- Flag case for manager review if pattern repeats

#### **Scenario B: Required Clinician Is Unavailable or Overbooked**
- *Reason:* "PT has full schedule," "SLP is on vacation," "No backfill available"

**Action:**
1. Immediately notify **Scheduling Supervisor**
2. Scheduling Supervisor will:
   - Check all available clinicians in that discipline across agency
   - Offer overtime/additional hours if necessary
   - Contact clinical manager if workload imbalance exists

**If Still Unschedulable:**
- Escalate to **Clinical Supervisor + Scheduling Manager (joint decision)**
- Determine if patient discharge is appropriate or if LUPA acceptance is necessary

#### **Scenario C: Clinically Uncertain (Patient Plateau or Declining)**
- *Reason:* "Patient not improving; unclear if visit is still necessary"

**Action:**
1. **Escalate to Clinical Supervisor immediately**
2. Clinical Supervisor will:
   - Review patient chart and recent progress notes
   - Contact ordering physician to reassess appropriateness of visit frequency
   - May recommend discharge if rehabilitation goals are met

**Do NOT automatically schedule if clinical value is unclear.** Let Clinical Supervisor decide.

## WHEN TO CONTACT SUPERVISORS

### Contact Scheduling Supervisor When:
- A clinician is unavailable and you cannot find backfill
- You need to override conflicting schedules
- Patient contact attempts are unsuccessful (>2 attempts)
- You suspect clinician capacity/workload issue (e.g., Angela Torres)

### Contact Clinical Supervisor When:
- Patient declines or is unavailable for visit
- Clinical appropriateness is uncertain
- Physician frequency adjustment may be needed
- You suspect premature discharge or safety concern

### Contact Both Supervisors (Joint Call) When:
- LUPA risk cannot be prevented despite all efforts
- Decision needed on accepting LUPA vs. forcing visit vs. discharging patient
- Clinician performance issues are blocking visit scheduling

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: What if patient is in the hospital or facility?**
A: Contact the hospital case manager or facility coordinator. Coordinate with RN to schedule visit while patient is still there (or arrange for post-discharge visit immediately upon return home).

**Q: Can telehealth count toward LUPA minimum?**
A: Yes, IF the physician POC indicates telehealth is appropriate. Check the Plan of Care; if it says "telehealth permitted," you can schedule via telehealth. Document the modality in the visit note.

**Q: What if the 30-day cycle ends before I can schedule the visit?**
A: Once the 30-day cycle ends, the LUPA calculation is frozen. A new 30-day cycle begins, and the patient starts fresh. If LUPA occurred in the prior period, it's recorded; focus then shifts to preventing LUPA in the new period. Notify billing so they process the LUPA adjustment.

**Q: If a patient is discharged, does that count as LUPA?**
A: Not if the discharge is clinically appropriate and documented. Ensure discharge summary includes clinical reason. If discharged before minimum visits, it's not a penalty—it's clinical decision. Document the reason clearly.

**Q: What if the ordering physician says the patient doesn't need more visits?**
A: If physician reduces frequency to below-LUPA threshold, that's a POC modification and is acceptable. Document the physician order change in EMR. No LUPA penalty applies if orders are updated.

**Q: Can the patient get visits from a different agency to prevent LUPA?**
A: No. LUPA is calculated per agency. If patient transfers agencies, each agency calculates LUPA based on their own visits. Focus on retaining patients and delivering their ordered visits.

**Q: What's the time frame to prevent LUPA if I get the flag?**
A: Act within 24 hours of receiving the notification. LUPA is calculated at end of 30-day period, but to prevent it, visits must be completed before that date. Usually you have 5–7 days remaining when you get the flag.
