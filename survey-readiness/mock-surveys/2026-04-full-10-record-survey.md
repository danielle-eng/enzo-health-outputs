### Finding #1 — Incomplete Comprehensive Assessment Documentation

**CoP Section:** § 484.55(b) — Comprehensive Assessment Requirements
**Tag Number:** F-tag 655
**Severity:** D-level (No Actual Harm, Potential for More Than Minimal Harm)
**Patient:** PT002 (Margaret D.)

**Finding:**
PT002's comprehensive assessment documentation lacks required OASIS-specific elements and structured assessment framework. ### Finding #2 — Missing Structured Plan of Care with Measurable Goals

**CoP Section:** § 484.60(a)(1) — Plan of Care Requirements
**Tag Number:** F-tag 677
**Severity:** D-level (No Actual Harm, Potential for More Than Minimal Harm)
**Patient:** PT002 (Margaret D.)

**Regulatory Requirement:**
42 CFR § 484.60(a)(1) requires plan of care established and reviewed by physician in collaboration with other health professionals, based on comprehensive assessment, addressing all identified problems/diagnoses, and including specific measurable goals with interventions needed.

**Finding:**
PT002's plan of care lacks required structure with measurable goals and physician authorization. The note states "Continue current skilled nursing visits for wound care. Monitor wound for signs of infection. Continue current medication regimen" which is task-based rather than goal-oriented. Specific deficiencies: (1) No measurable goals with timelines (e.g., "Wound will reduce to ≤1.0 cm x 1.5 cm by 2026-04-24"); (2) No goals for respiratory/COPD management or diabetes management despite multiple diagnoses; (3) No physician order verification or signatures; (4) No timeline for visits or expected duration of skilled services; (5) No discharge criteria identified.

**Evidence:**
- Plan section contains only task list: "Continue current skilled nursing visits for wound care. Monitor wound for signs of infection. Continue current medication regimen. Patient instructed to call if any concerns develop."
- No reference to physician involvement in plan review
- No goal statements using measurable, time-bound language
- No assessment of whether current plan aligns with documented diagnoses and patient needs

**Severity Justification:**
Non-structured care planning without measurable goals represents potential for more than minimal harm in that clinicians cannot assess patient progress appropriately, may not recognize when to modify or discontinue services, and fail to coordinate with physician regarding plan adequacy.

**Recommendation:**
Implement structured Plan of Care template with required elements: (1) each identified problem/diagnosis with physician signature and date; (2) at least one measurable, time-bound goal for each problem (e.g., "Wound will heal to ≤1 cm x 1.5 cm by 2026-04-24"); (3) specific skilled nursing interventions with frequency and rationale; (4) physician order verification with signature; (5) visit frequency/duration with expected total episode length; (6) discharge criteria. ### Finding #9 — Incomplete Dysphagia Assessment Documentation (PT008)

**CoP Section:** § 484.55(b) — Comprehensive Assessment
**Tag Number:** F-tag 655
**Severity:** D-level (No Actual Harm, Potential for More Than Minimal Harm)
**Patient:** PT008 (Margaret N.)

**Regulatory Requirement:**
42 CFR § 484.55(b) requires comprehensive assessment of patient status, including assessment of all conditions affecting care and safety. For patients with dysphagia, comprehensive assessment must include formal swallowing evaluation with documentation of findings and recommendations.

**Finding:**
PT008's SLP assessment note (2026-04-04) documents clinical swallowing evaluation but reveals a critical gap: the patient was discharged from acute hospitalization 8 days prior to SLP evaluation without formal swallowing assessment documentation. The note states "Complete formal swallowing evaluation (Modified Barium Swallow Study) has NOT been performed by SLP in inpatient setting (per available records). Current assessment based on clinical observation and patient history." This represents a significant gap in acute care coordination. Additionally, the home health SLP note, while thorough in documenting findings on 2026-04-04, lacks documentation of: (1) baseline dysphagia severity per established rating scale; (2) specific recommendations for rehabilitation timeline; (3) documented plan for formal VFSS if clinical assessment indicates need.

**Evidence:**
- PT008 visit note (2026-04-04): Documentation explicitly states no formal swallowing evaluation was performed in acute setting
- Patient discharged from acute care without SLP evaluation despite recent stroke with observed swallowing difficulty
- Home health SLP must perform comprehensive evaluation retroactively (8 days post-discharge)
- Home health assessment is first formal SLP documentation in patient's record

**Severity Justification:**
Absence of formal swallowing assessment in acute care and incomplete initial home health assessment (lacking baseline severity rating and rehabilitation timeline) represents potential for more than minimal harm. Dysphagia post-stroke requires skilled assessment to prevent aspiration pneumonia. Gap between acute hospitalization and home health SLP assessment (8 days) created period of inadequate monitoring.

**Clinical Implication of Gap:**
This finding highlights a systemic issue: coordination between acute care and home health may not ensure timely SLP referral for stroke patients with documented dysphagia. The patient was at risk for aspiration pneumonia during the 8-day gap without formal swallowing assessment.

**Recommendation:**
This gap reflects an acute care coordination issue beyond home health agency's direct control. However, home health can implement: (1) Protocol requiring SLP evaluation within 48 hours of admission for all stroke patients; (2) Review of acute discharge summaries to identify patients with dysphagia or "swallowing difficulty" documented; (3) Recommendation to physicians for formal VFSS if clinical assessment indicates need; (4) Documentation of formal dysphagia severity rating (penetration-aspiration scale or similar) at initial SLP evaluation. Recommend communication with referring hospital regarding need for timely SLP referrals for stroke patients prior to discharge.

**Target Completion:** 2026-05-03 (30 days for home health protocol implementation)

### Finding #13 — Boilerplate Homebound Justification Without Patient-Specific Clinical Detail (PT010)

**CoP Section:** § 484.110(a) — Clinical Records Content
**Tag Number:** F-tag 693
**Severity:** B-level (No Actual Harm, Potential for Minimal Harm)
**Patient:** PT010 (Edward S.)

**Regulatory Requirement:**
42 CFR § 484.110(a) requires patient's clinical record contain sufficient information to justify admission and document patient's status.

**Finding:**
PT010's PT visit note states as homebound justification: "Patient is unable to leave home without taxing effort." This generic statement lacks patient-specific clinical detail required to justify homebound status for Medicare/home health eligibility. The statement could apply to virtually any home health patient. Missing: (1) specific clinical explanation for why patient cannot leave home (specific COPD symptoms, oxygen requirements, activity limitations); (2) timeframe (8 weeks into episode per header information, yet homebound statement shows no progression or improvement documentation); (3) documented functional limitations with objective detail (e.g., "Patient unable to ambulate more than 50 feet before requiring 15-minute rest due to dyspnea" vs. "unable to leave home without taxing effort"); (4) assessment of whether patient remains homebound or if status is changing.

**Evidence:**
- PT010 visit note (2026-04-04): Homebound section contains only: "Patient is unable to leave home without taxing effort."
- This generic statement lacks patient-specific clinical detail
- In contrast, PT001's homebound statement provides specific clinical explanation: "Patient is unable to leave residence without considerable/taxing effort due to dyspnea on minimal exertion (DOE) and lower extremity edema. Patient reports needing to rest after ambulating 15 feet within the home..."
- PT010 note shows lack of clinical specificity throughout (minimal treatment detail, generic functional status observations)

**Severity Justification:**
While B-level (minimal harm potential), generic homebound statements create documentation risk if audited. Homebound justification documentation should be patient-specific with clear clinical rationale. Generic statements could be challenged by Medicare/auditors as insufficient to justify home health admission eligibility.

**Recommendation:**
Implement requirement that homebound status documentation includes: (1) specific diagnosis(es) limiting community ambulation; (2) specific functional limitation (e.g., dyspnea on exertion distance/duration, pain limiting activity, balance impairment, oxygen dependency); (3) medical necessity for assistance to leave home; (4) assessment of whether status is stable or changing. Provide PT staff training with specific example template: "Patient is homebound due to COPD with dyspnea on minimal exertion. Patient unable to ambulate beyond 50 feet without rest due to shortness of breath. Patient requires supplemental oxygen with any community activity. Patient dependent on wife for assistance exiting home safely due to balance precautions."
