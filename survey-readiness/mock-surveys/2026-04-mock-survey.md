## Statement of Deficiencies

### Finding #1 — Incomplete Comprehensive Assessment Documentation

**CoP Section:** § 484.55(b) — Comprehensive Assessment Requirements
**Tag Number:** F-tag 655
**Severity:** D-level (No Actual Harm, Potential for More Than Minimal Harm)
**Patient:** PT002 (Margaret D.)

**Regulatory Requirement:**
42 CFR § 484.55(b) requires that the agency ensure a comprehensive assessment of the patient is completed using an OASIS tool or equivalent comprehensive assessment instrument, performed in person by a qualified healthcare professional within the timeframes specified in the regulations. The assessment must include past medical history, current medical status, social and environmental factors, and functional status using standardized instruments with all required elements documented.

**Finding:**
During record review of PT002's visit note dated 2026-04-03, the comprehensive assessment documentation lacks required OASIS-specific elements and structured assessment framework. The note contains vital signs and wound assessment but does not document assessment using the required OASIS standardized tool or equivalent comprehensive assessment instrument. Missing documentation includes: (1) past medical history detail (only brief mention of COPD and diabetes); (2) social/environmental assessment (living situation mentioned but no assessment of safety, support systems, or environmental barriers); (3) cognitive and emotional status assessment (no formal cognitive assessment documented); (4) equipment and supplies assessment; (5) quantified functional status measurements using standardized scales. The note relies on narrative description without OASIS or standardized tool structure.

**Evidence:**
- PT002 visit note (2026-04-03): Contains only vital signs summary (temp 98.2, BP 138/82, HR 82, RR 20, SpO2 94%, weight 142 lbs) without structured OASIS assessment
- No reference to OASIS-C2 assessment or equivalent comprehensive assessment tool
- Functional status section states "Patient able to ambulate around the home with minimal assistance" without quantified measures
- Cognitive status not formally assessed; no documentation of orientation, alertness, or memory
- Social/environmental section minimal: "Lives with daughter" without assessment of home safety, equipment needs, or support system adequacy
- Missing documentation of pain, skin condition risk, and nutritional status in structured format

**Recommendation:**
Implement standardized OASIS-C2 (or agency-approved equivalent) comprehensive assessment tool for all initial and comprehensive assessment visits. Train all clinical staff on required OASIS elements including: (1) medical history section completion with diagnosis detail; (2) psychosocial assessment including cognitive status, mood, and coping; (3) environmental assessment including home safety and equipment needs; (4) functional status using OASIS-specific items with quantified responses; (5) clinical assessment items for all body systems including integumentary, respiratory, cardiac, GI, and GU. Implement audit process to verify all required elements documented before visit note is finalized. Target completion: 30 days for staff training; full compliance by 2026-05-03.

### Finding #2 — Incomplete Plan of Care Alignment and Documentation

**CoP Section:** § 484.60(a)(1) — Plan of Care Requirements
**Tag Number:** F-tag 677
**Severity:** D-level (No Actual Harm, Potential for More Than Minimal Harm)
**Patient:** PT002 (Margaret D.)

**Regulatory Requirement:**
42 CFR § 484.60(a)(1) requires that the plan of care is established, reviewed, and revised by a physician, in collaboration with other health professionals involved in the care, as needed. The plan must be based on the comprehensive assessment, address all identified patient problems/diagnoses, and include specific measurable goals with interventions needed. Plan of care must be documented and updated based on clinical findings.

**Finding:**
During record review of PT002's visit note dated 2026-04-03, the plan of care is not documented in structured format with measurable goals and interventions. The note states "Continue current skilled nursing visits for wound care. Monitor wound for signs of infection. Continue current medication regimen." This represents task-based documentation rather than goal-oriented plan of care. Specific deficiencies: (1) No measurable goals established for wound healing (no target healing timeline, no specific wound size reduction goals); (2) No goals for respiratory/COPD management (no specific oxygenation or functional targets); (3) No goals related to diabetes management despite Metformin documented in medication list; (4) No physician order verification or signatures; (5) Plan does not reflect any assessment findings or clinical decision-making process; (6) No timeline for visits or expected duration of skilled nursing services; (7) No discharge criteria identified.

**Evidence:**
- PT002 visit note (2026-04-03): Plan section contains only: "Continue current skilled nursing visits for wound care. Monitor wound for signs of infection. Continue current medication regimen. Patient instructed to call if any concerns develop."
- No reference to physician orders or physician involvement in plan review
- No goal statements using measurable, time-bound language (e.g., "wound will reduce to 1 cm x 1.5 cm within 2 weeks")
- No assessment of whether clinical findings support continued skilled services or modification of care plan
- Wound assessment (2 cm x 2.5 cm x 1 cm depth with erythema) documented but no plan modification despite findings
- No documentation of whether current plan aligns with COPD exacerbation diagnosis

**Recommendation:**
Implement structured Plan of Care template with required elements: (1) each identified problem/diagnosis with physician signature and date; (2) at least one measurable, time-bound goal for each problem (e.g., "Wound will heal to ≤1 cm x 1.5 cm by 2026-04-24"); (3) specific skilled nursing interventions with frequency and rationale; (4) physician order verification with signature; (5) visit frequency/duration with expected total episode length; (6) discharge criteria. Require all RNs to complete plan of care documentation training. Implement weekly peer review of plan of care documentation for first 60 days, then monthly audit. Target completion: 30 days.

### Finding #3 — Inadequate Clinical Record Completeness and Detail

**CoP Section:** § 484.110(a) — Clinical Records Content
**Tag Number:** F-tag 693
**Severity:** D-level (No Actual Harm, Potential for More Than Minimal Harm)
**Patient:** PT003 (Robert K.)

**Regulatory Requirement:**
42 CFR § 484.110(a) requires that the patient's clinical record contains sufficient information to justify admission to the agency, to document the clinical course and response to treatment, and to facilitate continuity of care. Records must be complete, accurate, legible, and include all required elements including assessment findings, interventions, patient response, and plan modifications.

**Finding:**
During record review of PT003's physical therapy visit note dated 2026-04-03, the clinical record is severely deficient in content and detail, falling far short of standards required to justify skilled service delivery or document clinical course. Specific deficiencies: (1) Visit summary contains only one generic sentence: "Patient cooperative during today's session. Exercises performed. Patient tolerating therapy well. Mobility improved. Continue with current plan." with no objective assessment of baseline or current status; (2) Treatment section provides no detail on specific exercises performed, number of repetitions, duration, or modifications made; (3) No vital signs documented despite post-surgical patient being 22 days post-hip replacement (on 2026-04-03, 22 days post-op assumed based on diagnosis); (4) No pain assessment documented despite hip replacement being high-pain procedure; (5) No ROM measurements or functional outcome measures documented; (6) No assessment of incision/surgical site; (7) No documentation of weight-bearing status or precautions; (8) No documentation of goals or progress toward goals; (9) Visit time documented as 15:00 but no duration provided; (10) No evidence clinical decision-making or skilled assessment occurred.

**Evidence:**
- PT003 visit note (2026-04-03): Total note length 41 lines with minimal clinical content
- Visit Summary: "Patient cooperative during today's session. Exercises performed. Patient tolerating therapy well. Mobility improved. Continue with current plan." (singular sentence)
- Treatment section: "Exercises done. Patient able to move around. No pain reported today. Treatment went okay." (4 sentences with no specificity)
- Plan section: "Continue PT visits. Exercises at home. Return in 2 days." (minimal documentation)
- No objective measurements of any kind
- No signature time documented (visit time 15:00 but signature time not recorded)
- Note provides no evidence that clinician performed skilled assessment or clinical decision-making; could apply generically to any PT patient

**Recommendation:**
Implement mandatory clinical documentation standards for all therapy visits including: (1) baseline and current status assessment with objective measures (ROM, strength, pain scale 0-10, functional mobility score); (2) specific exercises with sets, repetitions, and resistance; (3) vital signs (HR, BP, RR at minimum for post-operative patients); (4) surgical site/wound assessment for post-operative patients; (5) weight-bearing/precautions status; (6) progress toward established goals with evidence-based outcome measures; (7) modifications to plan with clinical rationale; (8) patient response to treatment; (9) signature with time. Require PT staff training on documentation standards and provide weekly documentation review for David Lee, PT for next 4 weeks with corrective feedback. Implement monthly audit of PT notes for compliance. Target completion: Immediate (retroactive note improvement within 2 days; future compliance by next visit).

### Finding #4 — Inadequate Skilled Care Justification and Physician Order Documentation

**CoP Section:** § 484.75(b) — Skilled Professional Services
**Tag Number:** F-tag 703
**Severity:** E-level (No Actual Harm, Potential for More Than Minimal Harm)
**Patient:** PT003 (Robert K.)

**Regulatory Requirement:**
42 CFR § 484.75(b) requires that all skilled services furnished are reasonable and necessary for the treatment of the patient's illness or injury, are based on a physician's plan of care and physician's orders, and are documented in the patient's clinical record. The record must contain sufficient detail to demonstrate the medical necessity for skilled service and justify the skilled level of care.

**Finding:**
During record review of PT003's physical therapy visit note dated 2026-04-03, the clinical record contains no evidence of physician orders, no justification for skilled PT services, and no documentation linking the services rendered to the patient's medical necessity or physician's plan of care. The note states only "Patient cooperative during today's session. Exercises performed" without documenting: (1) physician order authorizing PT or specific PT goals/precautions; (2) medical necessity for skilled PT at this post-operative stage (22+ days post-op assuming 2026-03-12 surgery); (3) clinical decision-making that requires PT skill (e.g., assessment of ROM progression, weight-bearing tolerance, pain management); (4) assessment of whether patient could safely perform exercises independently (criterion for skilled vs. non-skilled); (5) specific findings that justify continued skilled services; (6) plan modification or discharge planning based on patient progress.

**Evidence:**
- PT003 visit note (2026-04-03): Contains no reference to physician orders, plan of care, or medical necessity statement
- Treatment documentation: "Exercises done. Patient able to move around. No pain reported today. Treatment went okay." provides no evidence of skilled assessment
- No documentation of baseline ROM or pain that would justify comparison and establish medical necessity
- Plan states "Continue PT visits. Exercises at home. Return in 2 days." without clinical rationale or physician authorization
- Note does not address rehabilitation potential, discharge criteria, or patient's trajectory toward independence
- No documentation of skilled assessment (e.g., assessment of gait patterns, weight-bearing tolerance, ROM limitations, swelling assessment)

**Recommendation:**
Require documentation of physician order/authorization at start of each skilled PT episode. Implement mandatory inclusion in each PT note of: (1) physician order authorization with specific PT goals and precautions; (2) skilled assessment findings that distinguish skilled PT from unskilled exercise; (3) objective baseline and current ROM/strength measurements with comparison; (4) clinical decision-making rationale for continued skilled services; (5) assessment of patient's ability to perform activities safely and independently; (6) progress toward goals with specific functional improvements; (7) plan modification or discharge planning. Provide PT staff training within 5 days. Implement weekly peer review of PT documentation for David Lee, PT for 4 weeks. Target completion: 30 days for full compliance.

### Finding #5 — Absence of Required Wound Care Assessment Elements

**CoP Section:** § 484.55(b) — Comprehensive Assessment of Wound Status
**Tag Number:** F-tag 655
**Severity:** E-level (No Actual Harm, Potential for More Than Minimal Harm)
**Patient:** PT002 (Margaret D.)

**Regulatory Requirement:**
42 CFR § 484.55(b) requires comprehensive assessment including skin and wound status. For patients with wounds requiring skilled care, comprehensive documentation must include wound location, dimensions, depth, appearance (color, undermining, tunneling), drainage (type, amount, odor), surrounding skin condition, signs of infection, pain, and assessment of healing progress relative to baseline.

**Finding:**
During record review of PT002's wound care visit note dated 2026-04-03, the wound assessment lacks critical elements required for skilled wound care documentation and comprehensive assessment. The note documents: "Wound located on lateral left foot at site of prior pressure ulcer. Wound measured approximately 2 cm x 2.5 cm, depth approximately 1 cm. Slight erythema noted around wound edges. Minimal drainage present (serous). Surrounding skin warm to touch." Missing critical elements: (1) No baseline wound size documented for comparison to establish healing/deterioration trend; (2) No documentation of previous wound measurements for this episode; (3) No assessment of undermining or tunneling; (4) No pain assessment related to wound; (5) No assessment of wound type/stage classification (if pressure ulcer); (6) No specific wound care plan with product names/types; (7) No timeline for healing expectations; (8) No assessment of risk factors for non-healing (nutrition, mobility, moisture control); (9) No patient education documentation regarding wound care compliance; (10) Erythema noted as "slight" but no assessment of whether this represents improvement, stability, or concern.

**Evidence:**
- PT002 visit note (2026-04-03): Wound assessment section states "Wound measured approximately 2 cm x 2.5 cm, depth approximately 1 cm. Slight erythema noted around wound edges. Minimal drainage present (serous)."
- No previous wound measurements referenced for comparison
- Note does not document pressure ulcer staging or wound classification
- No pain scale assessment documented
- Dressing type/product not documented: "New dressing applied with sterile technique" but no specific dressing product name or type
- No assessment of surrounding skin condition beyond "warm to touch"
- No documentation of how "slight erythema" compares to prior visits or whether this is improvement/deterioration
- Drainage assessment vague: "minimal drainage present (serous)" without quantity or frequency assessment

**Recommendation:**
Implement standardized wound assessment template with required elements: (1) wound location with anatomical precision; (2) wound dimensions (length x width) with measurement from baseline for trend assessment; (3) depth assessment; (4) wound staging/classification; (5) appearance documentation (color, exposed structures, undermining, tunneling, granulation tissue %); (6) drainage (type, amount in mL if measured, frequency); (7) surrounding skin condition (erythema distance from wound, maceration, dryness); (8) pain assessment 0-10 scale; (9) specific dressing product with change frequency; (10) nutrition and mobility factors affecting healing; (11) comparison to baseline with clinical interpretation of trends; (12) assessment of healing trajectory and medical necessity for continued skilled service. Require all RNs to complete wound care documentation training within 10 days. Implement peer review of all wound assessments for 30 days. Target completion: 30 days.

### Finding #6 — Insufficient Medication Documentation and Assessment

**CoP Section:** § 484.55(b) — Comprehensive Assessment of Medication Status
**Tag Number:** F-tag 655
**Severity:** C-level (No Actual Harm, Potential for Minimal Harm)
**Patient:** PT002 (Margaret D.)

**Regulatory Requirement:**
42 CFR § 484.55(b) requires comprehensive assessment of medication status including current medications, dosages, frequencies, and patient response/tolerance to medications. When assessing medication, the clinician must document review of actual bottles, verification of adherence, and assessment of any side effects or adverse reactions.

**Finding:**
During record review of PT002's visit note dated 2026-04-03, the medication review section lacks detail and verification documentation. The note states: "Patient currently taking: Albuterol inhaler—twice daily as prescribed; Tiotropium inhaler—once daily; Lisinopril for blood pressure; Metformin for diabetes. Patient states medications are 'fine' and is taking them regularly. No new medication concerns noted." Deficiencies include: (1) No documentation of medication verification method (bottles examined, pharmacy records reviewed, patient/caregiver report only); (2) No dosage documentation for Lisinopril and Metformin (only inhaler doses provided); (3) No assessment of adherence beyond patient statement "taking them regularly"; (4) No assessment of side effects or adverse reactions despite medications known to have common side effects (Metformin GI effects, Lisinopril cough); (5) No assessment of therapeutic effectiveness (e.g., BP control goal not specified); (6) No interaction assessment despite patient on 4 medications; (7) Patient's casual statement "are 'fine'" is insufficient documentation of medication tolerance; (8) No documentation of inhaler technique assessment despite COPD patient requiring skill assessment.

**Evidence:**
- PT002 visit note (2026-04-03): Medication section uses direct quote "Patient states medications are 'fine' and is taking them regularly" as primary documentation
- No specific dosages documented for Lisinopril ("for blood pressure") or Metformin ("for diabetes")
- No systematic assessment of known side effects (Metformin GI side effects, ACE inhibitor cough, etc.)
- Patient's verbiage "doing better today" noted in Lung Assessment but not integrated into medication effectiveness assessment
- No inhaler technique return demonstration or assessment documented despite referring to this in Patient Education section

**Recommendation:**
Implement standardized medication review documentation requiring: (1) method of verification (bottles examined, pharmacy list reviewed, patient/caregiver report); (2) complete dosage, frequency, and indication for all medications; (3) adherence assessment with specific examples; (4) assessment of side effects using checklist of common side effects for each medication class; (5) assessment of therapeutic effectiveness with specific clinical indicators (e.g., "BP controlled within goal range," "SpO2 maintained >92%"); (6) drug interaction awareness statement; (7) patient/caregiver education on medication purpose and side effects; (8) direct assessment of inhaler technique for respiratory patients with return demonstration. Provide RN staff training within 10 days. Implement peer review of medication sections in all notes for 30 days. Target completion: 30 days.

### Finding #7 — Inadequate Documentation of Patient Education

**CoP Section:** § 484.55(b) — Comprehensive Assessment of Patient Knowledge and Education Needs
**Tag Number:** F-tag 655
**Severity:** C-level (No Actual Harm, Potential for Minimal Harm)
**Patient:** PT002 (Margaret D.)

**Regulatory Requirement:**
42 CFR § 484.55(b) requires assessment of patient's understanding of instructions, including education provided and patient's response. When patient education is provided, documentation must include: topic covered, content delivered, patient verbalization of understanding or return demonstration, and identified barriers addressed.

**Finding:**
During record review of PT002's visit note dated 2026-04-03, the Patient Education section contains minimal documentation with limited evidence of assessment of patient understanding or learning needs. The note states: "Discussed wound care and importance of keeping the wound clean and dry. Patient instructed to contact nurse if increased drainage or odor noted. Educated patient on importance of using inhalers regularly and on proper inhaler technique. Patient performed return demonstration of inhaler use and appeared to understand instructions." Deficiencies: (1) No documentation of patient's or daughter's barriers to learning or understanding; (2) Wound care education lacks detail of specific instructions given or patient's verbalization of understanding; (3) "Appeared to understand" is insufficient documentation—no specific patient statements or demonstration details recorded; (4) No assessment of patient's baseline knowledge or learning needs; (5) No documentation of whether caregiver (daughter) was included in or understood critical education topics; (6) No assessment of ability to perform wound care or medication management independently; (7) No documentation of follow-up plan for reinforcement of education.

**Evidence:**
- PT002 visit note (2026-04-03): Patient Education section: "Discussed wound care and importance of keeping the wound clean and dry. Patient instructed to contact nurse if increased drainage or odor noted. Educated patient on importance of using inhalers regularly and on proper inhaler technique. Patient performed return demonstration of inhaler use and appeared to understand instructions."
- Insufficient detail on wound care instructions (no specifics on dressing removal, cleaning technique, frequency)
- Vague language "appeared to understand" without specific patient verbalization documented
- Return demonstration for inhalers mentioned but details not provided
- No documentation of daughter's understanding despite her being identified as primary caregiver
- No assessment of COPD self-management education (oxygen use if applicable, activity tolerance, when to seek help)

**Recommendation:**
Implement standardized patient education documentation template requiring: (1) topic(s) covered with specific content detail; (2) identification of patient learning needs and barriers; (3) teaching method used (discussion, demonstration, written materials); (4) specific patient or caregiver verbalization of understanding (use direct quotes); (5) return demonstration with details of what was demonstrated and how successfully performed; (6) identification of any areas patient did not understand with plan for follow-up; (7) caregiver involvement with documentation of caregiver understanding; (8) written materials provided (name/date of material); (9) plan for reinforcement at next visit. Training for RN staff within 10 days. Peer review of education documentation for 30 days. Target completion: 30 days.
