## SYSTEMIC PATTERN GAPS (Issues in 3+ Records)

### Pattern Gap #1: Inadequate Skilled Care Justification Documentation
**PRIORITY: 🔴 CRITICAL — Systemic, High-Risk Finding**

**Gap Description:**
Skilled care necessity and physician order documentation is inconsistently or inadequately documented. Multiple records lack clear evidence that: (1) physician has ordered skilled services; (2) clinician has documented specific clinical findings that require skilled assessment; (3) medical necessity for continued services is established; (4) record demonstrates that patient could not safely perform care/exercises independently.

**Severity Distribution:** E-level (3 records), D-level (1 record across all CoP sections)

**CoP Section Violated:** § 484.75(b) — Skilled Professional Services Requirements

**Records Affected:** PT003, PT006, PT009 (3 records = 30% of sample)
- PT003: No physician orders documented; no skilled justification for continuing PT at week 6 post-op
- PT006: Minimal documentation of skilled assessment; no objective measures to distinguish skilled PT from unskilled exercise
- PT009: Medication non-compliance not assessed; no skilled assessment distinguishing nursing observation from care assistant observation

**Clinical Risk:**
Without documented skilled care justification, Medicare may deny payment for skilled services or identify as unnecessary duplication of care assistant services. For post-operative and acute patients, inadequate skilled assessment creates risk that patient complications go undetected. Each E-level skilled services deficiency creates claim denial risk and potential for harm.

**Root Cause Analysis:**
- **Likely Cause 1:** PT staff (David Lee, Michael Phillips) lack training on documenting skilled assessment vs. unskilled exercise
- **Likely Cause 2:** RN staff (David Martinez) may not understand that subjective patient report ("doing same as last visit") without objective assessment is insufficient for medical necessity justification
- **Likely Cause 3:** Possible workflow issue: physician orders may not be documented in notes; may be in separate physician order section of EHR that clinicians are not cross-referencing

**Systemic Impact:**
If this pattern continues across full patient census, agency could face significant compliance audit findings and claim denials. PT department particularly vulnerable given two PT clinicians (David Lee, Michael Phillips) involved in deficiency findings.

**Corrective Action:**

**Action 1 (Immediate - Within 2 Days):** Supervisory meetings with David Lee, PT and Michael Phillips, PT regarding deficient documentation pattern. Provide examples from PT003 and PT006 of what was missing and what should have been documented. Establish expectation for documentation standards going forward.

**Action 2 (Week 1):** Provide comprehensive training to PT staff covering:
- Definition of "skilled" PT vs. unskilled exercise per CMS guidelines
- Required documentation elements to justify continued skilled services: (a) physician order reference; (b) baseline and current objective measurements (ROM, strength, functional status); (c) clinical findings indicating patient cannot perform safely independently; (d) specific skilled assessment findings (e.g., assessment of pain, swelling, precautions); (e) progress toward goal or clinical justification for continued services
- Review of PT003 and PT006 as examples of deficient documentation with specific corrections needed
- Case study examples of adequate vs. inadequate skilled justification documentation

**Action 3 (Week 1):** Mandatory training for RN staff (particularly David Martinez) on medical necessity documentation for RN services. Emphasize that subjective patient report ("doing same," "feeling better") without objective clinical findings is insufficient. Required documentation: vital signs, objective assessments, skilled nursing assessment findings (not just vital signs).

**Action 4 (Week 1):** Audit current PT orders in EHR. Verify that physician orders exist for all active PT patients. Ensure that PT clinicians have access to physician orders and understand requirement to reference orders in documentation.

**Action 5 (Week 2):** Implement mandatory inclusion in all PT and RN notes of: (a) explicit reference to physician order authorizing services; (b) objective baseline/current assessment findings; (c) skilled assessment finding distinguishing from unskilled care; (d) clinical justification for continued services; (e) assessment of patient's ability to perform activities safely and independently; (f) progress toward goals.

**Action 6 (Week 2):** Develop standardized templates for PT documentation and RN documentation with required fields for: baseline ROM/functional status, current ROM/functional status, objective pain assessment, vital signs (where applicable), skilled assessment findings, plan modification rationale.

**Action 7 (Ongoing):** Implement weekly documentation peer review for all PT notes from David Lee and Michael Phillips for 4 weeks. Escalate any notes missing skilled justification elements to supervisor for immediate feedback. After 4 weeks, move to bi-weekly review through 2026-05-31.

**Action 8 (Ongoing):** Monthly audit of RN skilled services documentation (sampling 20-30% of RN visits) tracking: % with objective vital signs, % with skilled assessment finding documented, % with medical necessity explicitly addressed. Target 100% compliance by 2026-05-03.

**Owner:** PT Department Supervisor / Clinical Director
**Target Completion:** Full systemic compliance by 2026-05-03
**Monitoring Metrics:**
- % of PT/RN notes with physician order reference: target 100% by 2026-05-03
- % of PT/RN notes with objective baseline/current assessment: target 100%
- % of PT/RN notes with skilled assessment finding documented: target 100%
- Zero claims denied for "lack of skilled care justification" finding

**Risk if Not Addressed:** High. This is a high-risk compliance gap affecting 30% of sampled records. CMS surveyors routinely review skilled services justification. Multiple E-level deficiency findings across PT and RN services could trigger broader compliance concerns.

### Pattern Gap #2: Missing Objective Assessment Measurements and Clinical Trending
**PRIORITY: 🔴 CRITICAL — Systemic, High-Risk Finding**

**Gap Description:**
Multiple records lack objective measurements required for comprehensive assessment and clinical decision-making. Specific gaps vary by service type: PT notes missing ROM/strength/pain measurements; RN notes missing weight (CHF patients), edema grading, pain scales; SLP note missing formal dysphagia severity rating. Records contain only subjective observations without objective baseline and current measurements required for trending and assessing patient progress.

**Severity Distribution:** D-level and E-level findings

**CoP Sections Violated:** § 484.55(b) — Comprehensive Assessment; § 484.110(a) — Clinical Records

**Records Affected:** PT002, PT003, PT006, PT009 (4 records = 40% of sample)
- PT002: Missing baseline wound measurements for trending; no formal wound staging; no pain assessment
- PT003: Missing ROM/strength measurements; no pain assessment; no vital signs
- PT006: Missing ROM/strength measurements for comparison; no pain scale; no weight-bearing status
- PT009: **Missing weight documentation** (critical for CHF patient); no edema grading; no pain assessment

**Pattern Severity:** This pattern affects BOTH PT and RN services across different diagnosis types, suggesting systemic training need across all disciplines.

**Clinical Risk:**
Without objective measurements, clinicians cannot: (1) accurately assess patient progress toward goals; (2) identify when patient status is deteriorating or improving; (3) demonstrate medical necessity for continued skilled services; (4) provide adequate continuity of care information to next clinician. For CHF patient (PT009) missing weight, risk is particularly high: clinician cannot determine if diuretic therapy is working or if acute exacerbation is developing. For post-operative patients (PT003, PT006), missing ROM/pain measurements prevent assessment of post-operative recovery trajectory.

**Root Cause Analysis:**
- **Likely Cause 1:** Clinicians may not understand regulatory requirement for objective measurements in clinical records
- **Likely Cause 2:** Time constraints or workflow inefficiency may make comprehensive assessment feel burdensome; clinicians may feel subjective observation is sufficient
- **Likely Cause 3:** Measurement tools may not be readily available (weight scale access, ROM equipment, pain scale printing) or not built into workflow
- **Likely Cause 4:** Different clinicians may use different measurement approaches or may not have standardized forms

**Systemic Impact:**
Very high risk. 40% of sample records missing objective measurements. If this pattern holds across full census, agency has systemic documentation compliance risk. For CHF patients specifically, missing weight represents patient safety concern (unable to detect exacerbation early). CMS surveyor will specifically look for objective measurements and will cite if missing.

**Corrective Action:**

**Action 1 (Immediate):** For PT009 (Joseph M.) immediately document weight retroactively if available from prior visit weight trending, OR document in new visit note with explanation for missing measurement.

**Action 2 (Week 1):** Mandatory comprehensive training to ALL clinical staff (PT, RN, SLP, OT) on objective measurement documentation requirements covering:
- Regulatory requirement per § 484.55 and § 484.110
- Specific measurements required by discipline: PT (ROM, strength, pain, functional measures), RN (vital signs, pain, weight for CHF, edema grading), OT (ROM, functional status), SLP (dysphagia severity rating)
- How to baseline and trend measurements for clinical decision-making
- Case study examples from current records (PT003, PT006) showing inadequate vs. adequate documentation
- Practical troubleshooting: What to do if equipment not available? How to estimate if measurement tool not available?

**Action 3 (Week 1):** Audit current clinical tools and equipment. Verify availability of: PT weight scale (for PT009-type patients), ROM measurement devices, pain rating scale printouts/posters, functional outcome measure forms (e.g., for PT visits, OT activities of daily living measures). Ensure tools are accessible at each clinician's primary work location or in patient's home.

**Action 4 (Week 1-2):** Implement standardized measurement forms/templates for each service type with fields requiring documentation of:
- PT: baseline ROM (initial visit) and current ROM (each subsequent visit) with specific degree measurements, baseline pain scale (0-10) and current pain scale, baseline functional status and current status with specific measures (e.g., "ambulation distance: 50 feet at initial eval → 80 feet today")
- RN: vital signs (T, BP, HR, RR, SpO2) at each visit; weight at each visit (especially CHF patients); pain scale for any pain complaints; edema grading for lower extremity edema
- SLP: baseline dysphagia severity (penetration-aspiration scale or similar) with comparison to current severity

**Action 5 (Week 2):** EHR template update to include required fields for objective measurements with prompts if field left blank (e.g., "Warning: Weight not documented for CHF patient. Is weight assessment contraindicated or unavailable?").

**Action 6 (Ongoing):** Weekly peer review of all clinical notes for objective measurement documentation. Escalate any notes missing required measurements for that service type to clinician for addition (either retrospective addition or discussion of why measurement was not possible).

**Action 7 (Ongoing):** Monthly compliance metrics tracking for each service type:
- PT: % of notes with ROM measurements documented, % with pain scale, % with baseline/current comparison when indicated
- RN: % with weight documented (especially CHF patients), % with edema grading (when applicable), % with pain assessment (when indicated)
- SLP: % with formal dysphagia severity rating
- Target: 100% compliance for required measurements by service type by 2026-05-03

**Owner:** Clinical Director / Department Supervisors
**Target Completion:** Full systemic compliance by 2026-05-03
**Risk if Not Addressed:** CRITICAL. This affects 40% of sample and is core to regulatory compliance and patient safety. Weight missing in CHF patient represents both compliance and safety risk. CMS surveyors will specifically look for objective measurements.

### Pattern Gap #3: Incomplete or Missing Comprehensive Assessment Structure
**PRIORITY: 🟡 MODERATE — Systemic Issue**

**Gap Description:**
Assessment documentation lacks required structure, standardized tool usage (OASIS), or does not document all required assessment elements. Records may contain some clinical information but lack systematic approach to comprehensive assessment required by regulation. Affects mainly RN assessments and includes: missing OASIS framework, incomplete functional status assessment, missing or incomplete social/environmental assessment, missing cognitive status assessment.

**Severity Distribution:** D-level (3 records across multiple CoP sections)

**CoP Sections Violated:** § 484.55(b) — Comprehensive Assessment Requirements

**Records Affected:** PT002, PT004 (documentation adequate but lacks goal-specific language for ADL training), PT005 (overall good but isolated medication name missing)
**Note:** PT006, PT009 also contribute to this pattern with minimal overall assessment documentation

**Pattern Assessment:** 2-3 primary records (PT002, PT004) + contributing documentation inadequacy in 2-3 others (PT006, PT009)

**Clinical Risk:**
Non-standardized assessment may result in missing critical clinical information required for safe care. Without structured assessment, clinicians may overlook important findings, resulting in incomplete care planning and potential failure to identify acute changes.

**Root Cause Analysis:**
- **Likely Cause 1:** OASIS assessment tool may not be built into EHR in accessible format, or clinicians may not understand OASIS requirements
- **Likely Cause 2:** Clinicians may not have received training on comprehensive assessment requirements or OASIS-C2 elements
- **Likely Cause 3:** Possible workflow issue: OASIS may be completed in separate form not linked to visit note; clinicians may not understand that both assessments needed in clinical record

**Systemic Impact:**
Affects assessment quality across multiple records. While not as high-risk as skilled services justification pattern, this is core regulatory requirement affecting assessment-based deficiencies in multiple CoP sections.

**Corrective Action:**

**Action 1 (Week 1):** Audit current OASIS usage. Verify whether OASIS-C2 assessments are being completed for all patients as required. Determine where OASIS documents are stored and whether they are linked to patient clinical records in EHR.

**Action 2 (Week 1):** Mandatory training for all RN staff on OASIS-C2 assessment requirements covering:
- All required OASIS elements: (a) medical history detail; (b) cognitive/emotional status; (c) functional status with standardized measures; (d) environmental/safety assessment; (e) equipment/supplies assessment; (f) pain assessment
- How to document OASIS assessment in clinical note (vs. separate form): bridge between OASIS completion and note documentation
- Case study (PT002) of deficient OASIS assessment with corrections needed

**Action 3 (Week 2):** Implement standardized assessment template in EHR that includes prompts for all required OASIS elements and ensures clinicians complete all sections before note can be saved.

**Action 4 (Ongoing):** Weekly peer review of all RN initial/comprehensive assessments for OASIS element completeness. Escalate incomplete assessments to RN for correction.

**Action 5 (Ongoing):** Monthly audit (sample 20-30% of assessments) tracking: % with OASIS reference, % with all required elements present, % with documented baseline measurements. Target 100% by 2026-05-03.

**Owner:** Clinical Director / RN Supervisor
**Target Completion:** Full systemic compliance by 2026-05-03

## MODERATE PATTERN GAPS (Affecting 2-3 Records — Systemic Training Need)

### Moderate Gap #4: Plan of Care Documentation Lacks Measurable Goals
**PRIORITY: 🟡 MODERATE — Systemic Issue**

**Gap Description:**
While most records include some form of plan/goals, documentation frequently lacks: (1) measurable, time-bound goals with specific target outcomes; (2) physician signature and date on plan; (3) visit frequency/duration documentation; (4) clear connection between goals and assessment findings.

**Severity Distribution:** D-level (1 record: PT002)

**CoP Section Violated:** § 484.60(a)(1) — Plan of Care Requirements

**Records Affected:** PT002 (clear deficiency), PT004 (goals present but lack specific measurable targets), PT010 (adequate but general)

**Pattern Assessment:** Primary deficiency in PT002; contributing inadequacy in PT004

**Root Cause Analysis:**
- Clinicians may not understand goal-setting best practices (SMART goals: specific, measurable, achievable, realistic, time-bound)
- Possible EHR workflow issue: POC may be completed in separate system not integrated with visit notes
- May not have standardized POC template with required elements

**Corrective Action:**

**Action 1 (Week 1):** Training for all clinical staff on measurable goal development. Teach SMART goal format with examples:
- POOR: "Wound will heal" (not measurable or time-bound)
- GOOD: "Wound will reduce to ≤1.0 cm x 1.5 cm depth and show pink/red granulation tissue by 2026-04-24"
- POOR: "Improve mobility"
- GOOD: "Patient will ambulate 100 feet with single-point cane and contact guard assistance (from current 50 feet with moderate assistance) by 2026-05-04"

**Action 2 (Week 1-2):** Implement standardized POC template with required fields: (1) identified problems/diagnoses; (2) at least one SMART goal per problem; (3) specific interventions with frequency; (4) physician order verification; (5) visit frequency/duration; (6) expected episode duration; (7) discharge criteria.

**Action 3 (Ongoing):** Weekly peer review of all new POCs for goal quality. Return goals that are not measurable/time-bound to clinician for revision before implementation.

**Owner:** Clinical Director
**Target Completion:** 2026-05-03

### Moderate Gap #5: Inadequate Caregiver Assessment and Training Documentation
**PRIORITY: 🟡 MODERATE — Systemic Issue (Patient Safety Component)**

**Gap Description:**
Records inadequately document: (1) caregiver identification and capability assessment; (2) caregiver training with demonstration of competency; (3) assessment of caregiver knowledge and understanding of care requirements; (4) identification of barriers to effective caregiving. This is particularly important for high-risk patients (dysphagia, wound care, medication management).

**Severity Distribution:** C-level (1 primary: PT008), D-level contributing factor (PT002, PT004)

**CoP Sections Violated:** § 484.55(b) — Assessment; § 484.75(b) — Skilled Services (caregiver training component)

**Records Affected:** PT002 (mentions daughter but no caregiver assessment), PT004 (wife mentioned; training documented but lacks formal competency assessment), PT008 (husband training documented but lacks return demonstration of safety skills), PT009 (daughter mentioned but no assessment of her capability or role)

**Pattern Assessment:** Pervasive across records with identified caregivers. Affects 4 of 10 records (40%).

**Clinical Risk:**
Inadequate caregiver assessment and training creates patient safety risk. For PT008 (dysphagia patient), lack of formalized caregiver competency assessment means caregiver may not be feeding patient safely despite apparent verbal understanding. For PT004 (post-stroke patient), wife assisting with ADLs without formal assessment of her understanding of safety precautions (left-sided neglect awareness, fall prevention).

**Root Cause Analysis:**
- Clinicians may not be routinely assessing caregiver as part of comprehensive assessment
- Return demonstration requirement may not be clearly understood
- Template may not have dedicated caregiver assessment section

**Corrective Action:**

**Action 1 (Week 1):** Training for all clinical staff on caregiver assessment requirements. Define what constitutes adequate caregiver assessment vs. just mentioning caregiver exists:
- Required elements: (a) caregiver identification and relationship; (b) caregiver capability assessment (health status, ability to perform care tasks); (c) caregiver knowledge/understanding assessment; (d) formal return demonstration of safety-critical skills (for dysphagia, wound care, medication); (e) identification of backup caregiver; (f) assessment of caregiver burden/support needs

**Action 2 (Week 1):** Implement standardized caregiver assessment section in comprehensive assessment template with required fields for: caregiver identification, caregiver capability, caregiver education needed, return demonstration documentation, backup caregiver information.

**Action 3 (Ongoing):** Weekly peer review of all initial assessments. For any patient with identified caregiver, verify that all caregiver assessment elements documented. Escalate incomplete caregiver assessments to clinician for completion.

**Owner:** Clinical Director
**Target Completion:** 2026-05-03
