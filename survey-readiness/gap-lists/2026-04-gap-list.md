### Gap #1: Missing OASIS Structured Assessment Framework

**Gap Description:** Patient PT002's comprehensive assessment documentation fails to utilize OASIS-C2 or equivalent standardized assessment instrument, relying instead on narrative documentation without structured elements required by regulation.

**CoP Section Violated:** § 484.55(b) — Comprehensive Assessment Requirements
**Severity:** D-level (No Actual Harm, Potential for More Than Minimal Harm)
**Records Affected:** PT002 (Margaret D.)
**Clinical Risk:** Non-standardized assessment may miss critical clinical information, resulting in incomplete care planning and potential failure to identify acute changes in patient status. OASIS data is required for outcome measurement and quality assessment.

**Corrective Action:**
- **Action 1 (Immediate):** Audit all existing patient records for current OASIS assessment documentation status; identify patients who lack proper OASIS assessment on file.
- **Action 2 (Week 1):** Provide mandatory training to all clinical staff (RNs, PTs) on OASIS-C2 assessment tool requirements with focus on PT002's deficiency areas: past medical history detail, social/environmental assessment, cognitive status, equipment needs assessment, and standardized functional measurements.
- **Action 3 (Week 2):** Implement clinical documentation template that integrates OASIS-C2 elements into agency EHR/note template to ensure structure.
- **Action 4 (Ongoing):** Establish weekly peer review for first month, then monthly audit of comprehensive assessments to verify all OASIS elements present and documented.

**Owner:** Clinical Director / Documentation Compliance Officer
**Target Completion Date:** 2026-05-03 (30 days from survey)
**Monitoring:** Monthly audit of 100% of comprehensive assessments for OASIS element completion; track compliance rate trending toward 100% by 2026-05-31.

### Gap #2: Absent Plan of Care Structure and Medical Necessity Justification

**Gap Description:** Patient PT002's plan of care lacks required structure with measurable goals, specific interventions, physician authorization, and medical necessity justification. Documentation is task-based rather than goal-oriented.

**CoP Section Violated:** § 484.60(a)(1) — Plan of Care Requirements
**Severity:** D-level (No Actual Harm, Potential for More Than Minimal Harm)
**Records Affected:** PT002 (Margaret D.)
**Clinical Risk:** Non-structured care planning without measurable goals may result in inability to assess patient progress, inappropriate service continuation or discontinuation, and failure to coordinate with physician. Lack of physician signature/verification creates compliance risk.

**Corrective Action:**
- **Action 1 (Immediate):** Establish POC audit for PT002: review current plan and revise to include measurable goals with timelines for wound healing, respiratory function, and diabetes management.
- **Action 2 (Week 1):** Develop standardized Plan of Care template with required elements: (a) all identified problems/diagnoses; (b) at least one measurable, time-bound goal per problem (e.g., "Wound will reduce to ≤1.0 cm x 1.5 cm depth by 2026-04-24"); (c) specific skilled nursing interventions with frequency/rationale; (d) physician order verification with date/signature; (e) visit frequency and expected episode duration; (f) discharge criteria.
- **Action 3 (Week 1-2):** Provide mandatory training to all RNs on plan of care development using updated template. Include case studies demonstrating difference between task-based and goal-oriented documentation.
- **Action 4 (Week 3):** Implement electronic workflow requiring physician review/signature for all POCs before clinical implementation (add automated reminder in EHR for unsigned POCs).
- **Action 5 (Ongoing):** Establish weekly peer review of all new POCs for first 4 weeks; escalate any unsigned POCs to Clinical Director within 24 hours; move to monthly audit thereafter.

**Owner:** Clinical Director / Physician Advisor
**Target Completion Date:** 2026-05-03 (30 days from survey)
**Monitoring:** Track % of POCs with measurable goals, % with physician signatures, % with visit frequency/duration documented; target 100% compliance by 2026-05-03.

**Clinical Risk:** Inadequate documentation makes it impossible to assess patient progress, justify continued skilled services, or identify complications. For post-operative hip replacement patient, missing pain/vital sign assessment creates risk of undetected post-operative complications. Minimal documentation provides no evidence of skilled PT assessment, creating auditing risk and potential claim denial.

- **Action 2 (Immediate - Within 2 Days):** David Lee to update PT003 note with required retrospective documentation including: baseline ROM (if available from prior visits), exercises performed with specific sets/reps, vital signs, pain assessment, post-operative precautions/weight-bearing status, progress toward goals, clinical decision-making rationale.

- **Action 4 (Week 2):** Implement weekly documentation review and feedback for David Lee for next 4 weeks with corrective coaching; use PT003 as reference example of deficient documentation.
- **Action 5 (Week 2):** Implement standardized PT note template in EHR with required fields for: baseline/current ROM, pain, vital signs, specific exercises, goals progress, skilled assessment, and clinical decision-making section.

### Gap #4: Incomplete Wound Assessment Documentation and Clinical Trending

**Gap Description:** Patient PT002's wound assessment lacks critical elements including baseline comparison, wound staging/classification, undermining/tunneling assessment, pain assessment, specific dressing product documentation, and nutritional factors affecting healing.

**CoP Section Violated:** § 484.55(b) — Comprehensive Assessment of Wound Status
**Severity:** E-level (No Actual Harm, Potential for More Than Minimal Harm)
**Records Affected:** PT002 (Margaret D.)
**Clinical Risk:** Incomplete wound assessment documentation prevents tracking healing trajectory, may result in missed complications (undermining/tunneling), and makes it difficult to justify medical necessity for continued skilled services. Lack of baseline comparison prevents assessment of whether wound is improving, stable, or deteriorating.

**Corrective Action:**
- **Action 1 (Week 1):** Audit PT002's clinical record for any prior wound measurements; establish baseline wound size/depth/characteristics for trending purposes. Establish wound healing baseline parameters.
- **Action 2 (Week 1):** Develop standardized wound assessment template with required elements: (a) location (anatomical precision); (b) dimensions with measurement baseline for comparison; (c) depth; (d) wound staging/classification; (e) appearance detail (color, granulation %, undermining, tunneling); (f) drainage (type, amount, frequency); (g) surrounding skin condition; (h) pain assessment 0-10; (i) specific dressing product/type with change frequency; (j) nutrition/mobility factors; (k) comparison to baseline with trend analysis; (l) clinical interpretation of healing trajectory; (m) assessment of continued medical necessity for skilled service.
- **Action 3 (Week 1-2):** Provide mandatory training to all RNs on wound assessment documentation using new template. Include case study from PT002 demonstrating deficiencies and corrected documentation example.
- **Action 4 (Week 2):** Implement updated wound assessment template in EHR with required fields; consider adding photographing protocol for visual tracking (if HIPAA-compliant).
- **Action 5 (Ongoing):** Peer review all wound assessments for 30 days to ensure all required elements documented before note finalization; escalate incomplete assessments to RN for revision. Move to weekly spot-check after 30 days.

**Owner:** Clinical Director / Wound Care Specialist
**Target Completion Date:** 2026-05-03 (30 days from survey)
**Monitoring:** Audit 100% of wound assessments for element completeness weekly for 30 days; track compliance trending toward 100% by 2026-05-03; implement ongoing monthly quality checks.

**Severity:** D-level (No Actual Harm, Potential for More Than Minimal Harm)
**Records Affected:** PT002 (Margaret D.)

Reliance on patient statement without verification method documentation creates risk of inaccurate medication list and potential harm from incorrect dosages or undisclosed medications.

**Corrective Action:**

- **Action 3 (Week 1):** Provide training to all RNs on medication review documentation requirements. Include emphasis on: differentiation between patient opinion ("are fine") vs. detailed clinical assessment, systematic side effect assessment, and documentation of verification methods.
- **Action 4 (Week 2):** Implement medication review template in EHR with fields for verification method, complete dosing, side effect checklist, and therapeutic effectiveness assessment.
- **Action 5 (Ongoing):** Peer review medication documentation for all notes for 30 days; escalate incomplete documentation for revision before note finalization. Move to monthly spot-check review after 30 days.

### Gap #6: Inadequate Patient Education Documentation

**Gap Description:** Patient PT002's Patient Education section lacks specificity regarding content delivered, uses vague language ("appeared to understand") instead of specific patient verbalization, does not assess caregiver understanding despite daughter being primary caregiver, and lacks documentation of learning barriers.

**CoP Section Violated:** § 484.55(b) — Comprehensive Assessment of Patient Knowledge and Education Needs
**Severity:** C-level (No Actual Harm, Potential for Minimal Harm)
**Records Affected:** PT002 (Margaret D.)
**Clinical Risk:** Inadequate education documentation makes it difficult to assess whether patient actually understood instructions or to plan effective reinforcement. Lack of caregiver documentation may result in inconsistent patient care if caregiver does not understand critical instructions.

**Corrective Action:**
- **Action 1 (Week 1):** Develop standardized patient education documentation template with required elements: (a) topic(s) covered with specific content detail; (b) teaching method (discussion, demonstration, written materials); (c) identification of patient learning needs/barriers; (d) specific patient/caregiver verbalization of understanding (use direct quotes); (e) return demonstration with specifics; (f) caregiver involvement and understanding documentation; (g) written materials provided (name/date); (h) plan for reinforcement at next visit.
- **Action 2 (Week 1):** Provide training to all RNs on education documentation emphasizing importance of specific patient statements vs. clinician interpretation ("patient verbalized understanding of fluid restriction by stating '1500 mL daily means 50 ounces'" vs. "patient understood education").
- **Action 3 (Week 2):** Implement education documentation template in EHR with required fields and prompts.
- **Action 4 (Ongoing):** Peer review education documentation for 30 days; identify notes with vague language and return to RN for revision. Move to monthly spot-check review after 30 days.

**Owner:** Clinical Director / Nursing Education Coordinator
**Target Completion Date:** 2026-05-03 (30 days from survey)
**Monitoring:** Audit patient education sections of all notes weekly for 30 days tracking: % with specific content documented, % with patient verbalization documented, % with caregiver documentation (when applicable), % with learning barriers assessed. Target 100% compliance by 2026-05-03.
