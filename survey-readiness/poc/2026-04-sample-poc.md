**Affected Individuals:** PT002 (Margaret D.), and any other patients lacking OASIS documentation

### Corrective Actions Taken for Affected Individual(s)

**For PT002 (Margaret D.):**

2. Document assessment including all required elements:
   - Detailed past medical history (COPD etiology, onset, hospitalizations; diabetes management; other conditions)
   - Complete current medical status with vital signs interpretation
   - Medication review with dosages and therapeutic effectiveness
   - Social and environmental assessment (living situation with daughter, home safety evaluation, equipment needs, support system adequacy)
   - Functional status using OASIS-specific items (dyspnea level, edema assessment, ambulation status, ADL independence levels)
   - Cognitive and emotional status assessment
   - Pain assessment
   - Skin/wound status assessment

3. Timeline: Complete within 5 business days (by 2026-04-10)
4. Responsible party: Michael Torres, RN with clinical director oversight

2. **Audit Results:** Document findings in separate audit report, identifying which patients require retrospective OASIS assessments and which require current OASIS assessments
3. **Corrective Action Plan for Other Affected Individuals:** For any identified patients lacking OASIS assessment, complete assessments according to timeline below

### Measures Taken to Prevent Recurrence

**1. Clinical Staff Training (Completed by 2026-04-11)**
- Mandatory training for all clinical staff (RNs, PTs) on OASIS-C2 assessment tool and documentation requirements
- Training includes:
  - Overview of OASIS-C2 tool structure and required elements
  - Hands-on practice with actual OASIS-C2 tool using case examples
  - CMS rationale for OASIS standardized assessment
  - Integration of OASIS elements into clinical thinking and documentation
  - Common documentation errors with corrective examples
- Training delivery: Group session plus one-on-one if needed
- Training compliance: 100% attendance required; completion verified by attendance log and post-training competency check
- Trainer: Clinical Director with OASIS certification

**2. Clinical Documentation Template Update (Implemented by 2026-04-12)**
- Integrate OASIS-C2 elements into agency's EHR/documentation system
- Update visit note template to include structured sections for:
  - Medical history (past and current)
  - OASIS-specific assessment items
  - Social/environmental assessment
  - Functional status measurements
  - Cognitive and emotional status
- Ensure template is available in all clinician workstations and is the default template for all patient visits

**3. Workflow Implementation (Effective 2026-04-12)**
- Require completion of all OASIS elements before visit note can be electronically signed/finalized
- System prompts will alert clinicians to incomplete OASIS sections
- Documentation cannot be submitted without OASIS section completion (system enforced)

**4. Peer Review and Quality Assurance (Implemented 2026-04-12, Ongoing)**
- Weekly peer review of 100% of comprehensive assessments for first 4 weeks
- Peer review focuses on: completeness of OASIS elements, accuracy of assessment, consistency with clinical observations
- Any incomplete assessments returned to clinician for revision before finalization
- After week 4, transition to monthly spot-check audit (20-30% of comprehensive assessments)
- Monthly audit reports trended to track compliance improvement

**5. Supervision and Accountability**
- Clinical director reviews all new comprehensive assessments weekly during implementation phase
- Clinicians receive feedback within 24 hours of note submission
- Positive feedback provided for compliant assessments; corrective coaching provided for deficiencies
- Documented feedback maintained in individual clinician files

### How Agency Will Monitor Compliance Going Forward

**Ongoing Monitoring (Starting 2026-04-12, Indefinite):**

1. **Weekly Audits (Weeks 1-4 of Implementation):**

2. **Monthly Audits (Weeks 5 and Beyond):**

5. **EHR System Reports:**

- Standardized wound assessment template with required elements:
  - **Wound Identification Section:**
    - Location (anatomical description, e.g., "lateral left foot," "lower back," "right hip")
    - Date of wound onset or diagnosis
    - Wound etiology (pressure, venous, arterial, diabetic, traumatic, other)
    - Pressure ulcer staging if applicable (Stage I, II, III, IV, unstageable, suspected deep tissue injury)

  - **Wound Measurement Section:**
    - Length x width in centimeters (measure longest and widest dimensions)
    - Depth in centimeters (measure deepest point)
    - Comparison to baseline with date of baseline measurement

    - Wound bed color and % of wound bed composition (granulation tissue %, slough %, eschar %, epithelialization %)

    - Odor present? Yes/no

  - **Surrounding Skin Section:**
    - Erythema: present/absent; if present, distance from wound edge in centimeters
    - Maceration: present/absent
    - Induration: present/absent
    - Temperature: normal, warm, cool
    - Skin integrity: intact, broken, excoriation

  - **Pain Section:**
    - Pain level 0-10 scale at rest

    - Dressing tolerance: patient tolerance, adhesion, any issues

  - **Healing Factors Section:**
    - Nutrition status: adequate/inadequate protein intake, caloric intake
    - Mobility status and bed mobility
    - Incontinence: yes/no; if yes, type and frequency
    - Other factors affecting healing (age, comorbidities, medications)

  - **Clinical Assessment Section:**
    - Healing trajectory: improving, stable, deteriorating
    - Estimated healing timeline (e.g., "expected closure by 2026-05-15 at current rate")
    - Signs of infection or concern: present/absent; if present, plan to address
    - Justification for continued skilled services: brief statement of why skilled wound care is medically necessary
