# Enzo Health: 30-Day Customer Onboarding Timeline

**For:** Home Health Agency Leadership
**Purpose:** See exactly what happens when you activate Enzo Health and what decisions you'll make at each milestone

---

## WEEK 1: Data Intake & Baseline Assessment

### Day 1 (Monday)

**Agency Does:**
- Designate QAPI Coordinator (single point of contact)
- Attend kickoff call with Enzo QAPI Specialist (1 hour)
- Confirm budget and contracting finalized
- Review data requirements checklist (patient admissions, OASIS, claim outcomes, clinical notes)

**Enzo Does:**
- Introduce system architecture and 7-agent model
- Review data security + HIPAA de-identification process
- Set expectations: data in by Wednesday; preliminary findings by Friday

**Decision Point:** Approve data export and assign QAPI Coordinator

---

### Day 2–3 (Tuesday–Wednesday)

**Agency Does:**
- QAPI Coordinator exports baseline data (prior 30–90 days of admissions)
- Prepares CSV/Excel files: patient demographics, OASIS, hospitalization flags, ED visits, diagnoses, clinical note scans
- Submits data to Enzo

**Enzo Does:**
- Begins data quality review
- Runs initial validation checks (missing fields, invalid dates, diagnosis mismatches)
- Flags any data quality issues for clarification

**Deliverable from Enzo:** Data Validation Report (email with feedback or approval to proceed)

**Decision Point:** Clarify any data gaps; confirm data quality

---

### Day 5–7 (Thursday–Friday)

**Agency Does:**
- Answer Enzo's data quality questions (if any)
- Gather any missing fields (e.g., if OASIS functional outcomes unavailable, flag it)
- Confirm readiness for preliminary findings meeting (Friday)

**Enzo Does:**
- Complete baseline analysis
- Calculate quality indicators vs. CMS benchmarks
- Identify 3–5 RED/YELLOW flags
- Score sample clinical notes (10–15 notes)
- Draft preliminary findings briefing document (1–2 pages)

**Deliverable from Enzo:** Preliminary Findings Brief (summary of baseline performance, top 3 opportunities, preliminary PIP recommendations)

**Decision Point:** Plan preliminary findings meeting (Friday afternoon or Monday)

---

## WEEK 2: Diagnostics & Preliminary Reporting

### Day 8–10 (Monday–Wednesday)

**Agency Does:**
- Schedule preliminary findings meeting with Clinical Director, VP of Operations, and QAPI Coordinator
- Review preliminary findings brief in advance
- Begin identifying root causes (internal discussion): "Why do we think our hospitalization rate is 24%? What's happening operationally?")

**Enzo Does:**
- Deep-dive analysis on RED flags
- Root cause hypothesis generation (based on data patterns and clinical expertise)
- Begin drafting formal quarterly QAPI report (Section 1: Executive Summary; Section 2: Quality Indicator Dashboard)
- Prepare decision briefings for meeting

**Meeting Prep:** Agency gathers internal data that might explain findings (e.g., patient acuity data, staffing turnover, EMR issues) to discuss with Enzo

---

### Day 12 (Wednesday)

**Meeting: Preliminary Findings Review**

**Attendees:** Enzo QAPI Specialist, Agency Clinical Director, VP of Operations, QAPI Coordinator

**Agenda (45–60 min):**

1. **Quality Baseline (10 min):** Enzo reviews dashboard. "You're at 24% hospitalization vs. 14.7% benchmark. This means 12 of your 50 patients were hospitalized preventably."

2. **Root Cause Discussion (15 min):** Enzo presents hypotheses; agency confirms or contests. "Possible causes: delayed vital sign monitoring, gaps in medication adherence, insufficient communication with PCPs. Do these match what you're seeing?" Agency shares internal context.

3. **Documentation Quality Snapshot (10 min):** Enzo shares scores from 10–15 sample notes. "We found 3 major gaps: missing skilled care justification, no patient understanding documentation, and no goal reference. These patterns suggest staff training need vs. systemic template issue."

4. **Survey Readiness Preliminary (5 min):** Enzo flags 5–7 anticipated compliance gaps based on sample records review. "We expect CMS would cite: missing OASIS structure, non-standardized assessment, inadequate POC goals, deficient PT documentation."

5. **PIP Discussion (10 min):** Enzo recommends 2–3 PIPs based on data. "Hospitalization is your #1 opportunity. A targeted PIP could recover $16.8K+ in HHVBP penalties within 6 months. We recommend starting immediately."

6. **Next Steps (5 min):** Confirm interest in pursuing PIP(s). Agree on Week 3 kickoff plan.

**Decision Points (Agency Must Decide):**
- Do we agree with the root cause analysis?
- Should we pursue the hospitalization PIP?
- Do we want Enzo's help developing the formal PIP, or will we develop internally?
- What's our timeline for corrective actions (aggressive vs. phased)?

---

### Day 14 (Friday)

**Agency Does:**
- Attend Enzo training on weekly tracker (30 min webinar)
- QAPI Coordinator downloads weekly tracker template
- Assign owner for each area (PIP sponsorship, weekly data submission, board reporting)

**Enzo Does:**
- Deliver formal Quarterly QAPI Report (Sections 1–6: Executive Summary, Quality Dashboard, Indicator Analysis, PIPs, Patient Safety, Governing Body Summary)
- Provide access to GitHub repo and Google Drive folder (shared workspace for reports and templates)
- Schedule Week 3 PIP kickoff call

**Deliverable from Enzo:**
- **Q1 QAPI Report (5–8 pages):** Benchmark comparison, RED flags, PIP recommendations, patient safety summary
- **Weekly Tracker Template:** CSV or Google Sheet for ongoing data submission
- **GitHub/Drive Access:** Read-only or editor access to shared workspace

**Decision Point:** Confirm PIP sponsorship; assign clinical director as primary PIP owner

---

## WEEK 3: Performance Improvement Project Initiation

### Day 15–17 (Monday–Wednesday)

**Agency Does:**
- Clinical Director reviews formal QAPI report in detail
- Gathers clinical team for root cause discussion: "Given our hospitalization rate is 24%, what's actually happening with our CHF/COPD patients? When do they decompensate?"
- Identifies potential interventions (brainstorm with nursing, therapists, care coordinators)
- Confirms resource availability for PIP implementation (staff time, budget for monitoring systems, training costs)

**Enzo Does:**
- Develops pre-draft PIP (Problem Statement, Root Cause Analysis, SMART Goal, 7 Specific Interventions, Measurement Plan, Timeline)
- Prepares PIP coaching presentation (explaining how to read/implement the plan)
- Gathers additional data if needed (comparing Sunrise's hospitalization cases to benchmark agencies)

**Agency Action:** Prepare questions and internal input for PIP development meeting

---

### Day 19 (Wednesday)

**Meeting: PIP Development Kickoff**

**Attendees:** Enzo QAPI Specialist (+ optional clinical consultant), Agency Clinical Director, Nursing Director, Care Coordination Lead, QAPI Coordinator

**Agenda (1–1.5 hours):**

1. **PIP Overview (10 min):** Enzo explains 7-intervention model. "Hospitalization PIPs typically address: 1) early warning sign protocols, 2) 24/7 triage access, 3) staff education, 4) patient education, 5) medication compliance, 6) PCP coordination, 7) data monitoring."

2. **Sunrise's Specific PIP (30 min):** Enzo presents 7 interventions tailored to Sunrise's data. Each intervention includes: what it is, who does it, how long (timeline), success metric. Example:
   - **Intervention 1 — 24/7 Clinical Triage Line:** RN on-call (evenings/weekends) available for symptomatic patient calls. Escalation authority to schedule next-day visits for high-risk diagnoses. Cost: $8K setup + $300/month stipend. Timeline: Implement by 4/18. Success metric: 80%+ of ED visits diverted to home health call.

3. **Resource & Budget Review (15 min):** Clinical Director confirms capacity. "Can we implement a triage line? Do we have on-call RN coverage? What's the budget?" Enzo adjusts interventions if needed (e.g., defer one intervention if resources constrained).

4. **Ownership & Timeline (15 min):** Assign an owner for each intervention. Agree on weekly check-in schedule. Confirm that PIP will be submitted to QAPI committee for approval by end of Week 3.

5. **Measurement & Success (10 min):** Review tracking plan. "We'll measure hospitalization rate monthly using your weekly submissions. Target: 22% by end of April, 20% by end of May, 16% by end of July."

**Output from Meeting:**
- **Finalized PIP Document** (8–12 pages) with 7 interventions, owners, timelines, measurement plan
- **Implementation Checklist** (week-by-week action items)
- **Board Briefing Slide** (1–2 slides summarizing problem, goal, and implementation plan for board approval)

**Decision Points (Agency):**
- Do we approve this PIP?
- Do we have budget for the recommended interventions?
- Can we commit to timeline (implement by 4/18)?
- Will we submit to QAPI committee for formal approval?

---

### Day 21 (Friday)

**Agency Does:**
- Clinical Director submits PIP to QAPI Committee for formal review/approval (typically includes board-level governance)
- Prepares for internal implementation kickoff next week
- QAPI Coordinator submits first weekly tracker (data through end of Week 3)

**Enzo Does:**
- Receives first weekly tracker submission
- Reviews for completeness and quality
- Provides feedback (typically within 24 hours)
- Begins Month 1 preliminary analysis (how are we tracking toward targets?)

**Deliverable from Agency:** First Weekly Tracker Submission (CSV or Google Sheet with patient admissions, hospitalizations, ED visits, OASIS SOC dates, clinical note counts)

**Decision Point:** QAPI Committee formally approves PIP; Clinical Director empowered to begin implementation Week 4

---

## WEEK 4: Operations & Rhythm Establishment

### Day 22–28 (Monday–Sunday)

**Agency Does:**

**Monday (Day 22):**
- PIP Implementation Kickoff meeting with all clinical staff involved in interventions
- Distribute 7 interventions; confirm owners; review timeline
- Example: "David (Nursing Director) is leading the 24/7 triage line. You have 2 weeks to set up on-call schedule, train backup nurse, and go live by 4/18."

**Tuesday–Thursday (Days 23–25):**
- Intervention 1 (24/7 Triage) setup begins
- Staff education on early warning signs (facilitated by Enzo or internal educator)
- Review of medications & protocols

**Friday (Day 28):**
- **First Monthly Clinical Huddle** (1 hour, all clinicians + QAPI Coordinator)
  - Review hospitalization trend (is it moving toward target?)
  - Review ED visits (any patterns?)
  - Discuss first-week PIP feedback: "What's working? What's hard?"
- **Enzo Coaching Call** (30 min): Enzo reviews Week 1 tracker data, provides early feedback on trajectory

**Enzo Does:**
- Deploys optional weekly coaching calls (if included in contract)
- Reviews first weekly tracker submissions for data quality
- Tracks PIP implementation progress (via agency report or direct observation)
- Begins drafting Month 1 Preliminary Report (informal summary of how Week 1 progressed)
- Prepares for Week 4 board presentation

**Standing Deliverables (Ongoing from Week 4):**
- **Every Monday:** Clinical documentation QA review (all notes from prior week scored; deficient notes flagged to manager)
- **Every Friday:** Weekly regulatory digest (CMS updates, coverage decisions, product implications)
- **Every Friday:** QAPI Coordinator submits weekly tracker (data through Thursday)

---

### Day 28 (Friday)

**Meeting: Month 1 Progress Check-In**

**Attendees:** Enzo QAPI Specialist, Agency Clinical Director, VP of Operations, QAPI Coordinator

**Purpose:** Confirm that Week 4 operations are sustainable; adjust if needed

**Agenda (30–45 min):**

1. **Week 4 Tracker Data (5 min):** Enzo shares snapshot. "Week 4 admissions: 12 patients. No new hospitalizations (good sign). 1 ED visit. SOC completion: 100% (on track)."

2. **PIP Progress (10 min):** Clinical Director reports. "24/7 triage line is live. We got 3 calls this week; 1 prevented an ED visit. Staff training on early warning signs completed Monday."

3. **Documentation Quality Trend (5 min):** Enzo shares early feedback. "First 20 notes scored: average 6.8/10. Down from baseline 6.2/10 (slight improvement). Coaching taking effect."

4. **Survey Readiness (5 min):** Enzo provides status on corrective actions. "You committed to training RNs on wound assessment and medication review by 4/18. How's that progressing?"

5. **Adjustments (10 min):** Any concerns or obstacles? "Documentation coaching is working but taking more time than expected. We'll extend implementation to 6 weeks instead of 4." Enzo adjusts plan.

6. **Board Presentation (5 min):** Confirm talking points for board review. "Here's your Month 1 summary: PIP launched on time, early data trending positive, staff engaged, survey readiness gap remediation underway."

**Decision Point:** Confirm Month 2 plan; adjust PIP interventions if needed based on early feedback

---

### Day 30 (Monday of Week 5)

**Board Presentation (Optional but Recommended)**

**Content:**
- Executive Summary: "We had a 24% hospitalization rate. We're implementing a 7-intervention PIP. Week 4 data shows early promise."
- 3-Slide Deck:
  1. Baseline problem (24% vs. 14.7% benchmark; -$24K HHVBP penalty)
  2. PIP overview (7 interventions, timeline, resource investment)
  3. Month 1 update (triage line live, staff training done, tracking on target, survey gaps being remediated)
- Q&A: Board confirms support; approves budget (if not already approved)

**Deliverable from Enzo:** Enzo provides 3-slide template; Agency customizes with Month 1 data

1. **Preliminary Findings Brief** (2-page summary of baseline performance)
2. **Quality Indicator Dashboard** (table showing baseline measures vs. benchmarks)
3. **Sample Clinical Note Scores** (10–15 notes graded with feedback)
4. **Weekly Tracker Template** (CSV/Google Sheet for ongoing data submission)
5. **GitHub/Google Drive Access** (shared workspace for reports, templates, tracking)

**Strategic Deliverables:**
1. **Quarterly QAPI Report** (5–8 pages with benchmark comparison, PIP recommendations, patient safety summary)
2. **PIP Development Support** (Enzo provides draft 7-intervention PIP; agency refines)
3. **Regulatory Gap List** (preliminary survey readiness assessment based on sample records)
4. **Board Summary Slide** (1-page overview for leadership approval)

**What Agency Has Accomplished by End of Week 2:**
- ✓ Baseline performance clearly defined (24% hospitalization vs. 14.7% benchmark)
- ✓ Root causes identified and discussed with Enzo
- ✓ PIP decision made (pursuing hospitalization reduction)
- ✓ Preliminary survey readiness assessment received
- ✓ Team aligned on Month 1 priorities and timeline
- ✓ Weekly tracking established (to monitor progress toward targets)

## Summary: The 30-Day Journey

| Week | Agency Action | Enzo Delivery | Decision Point |
|------|---------------|---------------|----------------|
| **Week 1** | Appoint QAPI Coord; submit baseline data | Data quality report; baseline analysis | Confirm data quality; approve data use |
| **Week 2** | Attend findings meeting; review report; meet on PIP | Quarterly QAPI Report; PIP draft; preliminary survey gap list | Approve PIP; confirm budget; assign owners |
| **Week 3** | Finalize PIP; QAPI committee approval; weekly tracker kickoff | PIP coaching; measurement plan; weekly tracking setup | PIP formally approved; implementation timeline confirmed |
| **Week 4** | PIP implementation kickoff; staff training; first tracker submission | Weekly QA review; monthly progress assessment; coaching calls | Confirm sustainability; adjust if needed |

**By Day 30:**
- Agency is operating on weekly rhythms (Monday QA review, Friday tracker submission, Friday regulatory digest)
- PIP is live with 7 interventions actively implementing
- Hospitalization trend is being monitored (Week 4 data showing early promise or identifying obstacles)
- Board has been briefed and approved the plan
- Survey readiness gap remediation is underway (4–6 week timeline to completion)
- Organization is aligned on shared quality goals and measurement

**ROI by Day 30:**
- **Clarity:** Agency knows exactly how it's performing vs. competitors
- **Direction:** 7-intervention roadmap with clear owners and timelines
- **Accountability:** Weekly tracking creates visibility into progress
- **Risk Mitigation:** Survey gaps being remedied before CMS shows up
- **Financial Path:** Clear model for $16.8K–$37K annual recovery through quality improvement
