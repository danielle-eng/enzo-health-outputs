# Enzo Health: Investor Summary (Seed/Series A)

## Page 1: The Opportunity

---

### Market Size & Problem

**Home Health Quality Compliance Market: ~$8–12B annually**

The home health industry serves 5+ million patients annually across 33,000 home health agencies (per CMS). Of these, 80–85% are penalized under HHVBP (Home Health Value-Based Purchasing) for quality performance below national benchmarks.

**Three Core Pain Points:**

1. **HHVBP Penalties:** Agencies are losing $1–4M/year in Medicare adjustments due to hospitalization, ED utilization, and functional outcome gaps. Current industry penalty rate: -2.5% to -6.0% payment adjustment. Average agency penalty: $40K–$120K/year.

2. **CMS Survey Citations:** Home health agencies face compliance audits every 3 years (42 CFR Part 484). Agencies with poor documentation face deficiencies ranging from $10K–$100K+ in remediation and referral loss. 51% of home health claims contain improper payment errors (per OIG), driving recoupments and exclusion risk.

3. **Care Quality Gaps:** Preventable hospitalizations (24%+ vs. 14.7% benchmark) cost agencies $120K–$180K/year in lost HHVBP adjustments PLUS direct acute care costs ($5K–$15K per hospitalization). Better care coordination prevents these, but agencies lack the data infrastructure to identify at-risk patients before decompensation.

---

### Why Now: Three Regulatory Tailwinds

1. **OASIS-E2 Mandatory (April 1, 2026):** New data collection instrument requires staff retraining, template updates, and compliance validation. Agencies scrambling to comply. CMS enforcement focus on data accuracy. Those who miss the deadline face immediate survey deficiencies.

2. **CY 2026 Home Health PPS Final Rule:** 1.3% aggregate payment reduction (-$220M industry-wide), plus four assessment items removed, three HHCAHPS measures eliminated, and four new quality measures added. Agencies must recalibrate their operations. Those with agile quality systems adapt faster; others face margin compression.

3. **Increased OIG Enforcement:** OIG's home health work plan (FY 2026) targets improper payments, documentation accuracy, and face-to-face requirements. Federal-State partnerships increasing audit frequency. Agencies without proactive compliance infrastructure face recoupment risk.

---

### The Problem Enzo Solves

**Current State:** Home health quality directors are overwhelmed. They manually:
- Pull data from 3–5 disparate systems (EHR, OASIS, claims, billing, scheduling)
- Grade clinical notes by hand (100+ per month)
- Track compliance gaps across 42 CFR Part 484 requirements
- Create PIPs from templates, manually entering patient data
- Monitor HHVBP performance with 3-month lag

**Result:** Reactive, delayed decision-making. Agencies discover problems post-hoc (bad documentation, survey deficiency, penalty adjustment) instead of preventing them.

**Enzo's Solution:** AI agents that automate the work a full-time quality director would do, at 1/10th the cost.

### How It Works (The Architecture)

**Data In:**
1. Agency uploads 30–90 days of patient data (structured: admission/discharge, diagnoses, OASIS, claim outcomes) + clinical notes (unstructured: visit notes)
2. De-identification pipeline removes PII; assigns internal patient IDs
3. Data validated against CMS/OASIS schema; flagged data quality issues escalated to agency

**Agents Work (Parallel Processing):**
1. QAPI agent pulls OASIS data + claims outcomes; calculates quality measures vs. CMS benchmarks; identifies red flags
2. Clinical QA agent ingests notes; scores each against 5 audit-readiness dimensions; flags deficiencies
3. Survey agent uses outputs from QAPI + Clinical QA; maps gaps to 42 CFR 484 requirements; generates mock survey findings
4. Regulatory agent monitors CMS website (daily) for new NCDs, LCDs, policy updates; translates impact to product requirements
5. Outcomes agent builds financial models (HHVBP sensitivity analysis); identifies highest-ROI improvement opportunities
6. Risk agent flags patients meeting high-risk criteria; recommends protocols

**Reports Out (to GitHub + Google Drive):**
1. All 33 outputs pushed to agency's GitHub repo (version-controlled)
2. Executive summaries pushed to agency's Google Drive (shareable with leadership/board)
3. Templates pre-filled with agency data (PIP templates, gap list, patient protocols)

**Workflow:** Agency uses reports to brief leadership, implement corrective actions, track progress weekly.

- OASIS-C2 and OASIS-E2 element mapping to quality measures and HHVBP scoring
- 500+ CMS coverage decisions (NCDs/LCDs) indexed by home health diagnosis
- Cannot be outsourced; must be maintained in-house with real-time updates

**2. Clinical Template Library:**
- 50+ pre-built templates (QAPI report, PIP, survey gap list, clinical note scoring rubric, patient protocols)
- Each template built from actual CMS audit findings, OIG guidance, and accreditation standards
- Proprietary scoring algorithms for documentation quality (5-domain system), risk stratification, benchmark comparison
- Assembled over 18 months of clinical research + home health subject matter expert review

**3. Agent Coordination Protocol:**
- Agents must pass outputs from one to another (QAPI → Survey readiness; Clinical QA → QAPI; Regulatory → Product roadmap)
- Protocol ensures consistency, prevents hallucination, validates data flow
- Requires deep knowledge of home health workflows AND healthcare AI safety
- Not available as off-the-shelf LLM; requires custom-built orchestration

**4. EHR Integration Roadmap (Differentiator in Wave 2):**
- Planned connectors to leading home health EMRs (Amedisys, MatrixCare, Epic HHA, Carepath)
- Direct data pull (no manual export) unlocks real-time QA feedback + automated workflow triggers
- Competitors will copy the agents; hard to copy EMR integrations (requires vendor negotiation + custom API work)
