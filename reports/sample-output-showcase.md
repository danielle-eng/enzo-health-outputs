## Output #1: Your Q1 Quality Performance Report

**What it is:** Benchmark comparison report showing exactly how your agency performs on 8 quality measures vs. national CMS standards. This is your baseline—the first number in every conversation about improvement.

**Why it matters:** Most home health agencies are flying blind on quality performance until surveyors show up. This report tells you (months in advance) whether you're trending toward penalties, high survey risk, or strong performance.

**Real data from Sunrise:**
- Hospitalization rate: 24.0% (vs. 14.7% benchmark) — **9.3 points above target**
- Timely assessment: 92.0% (vs. 96.3% benchmark) — **4.3 points below target**
- Ambulation improvement: 48.0% (vs. 53.2% benchmark) — **near target**

**ROI:** **HIGH** — This single report clarifies whether you need a hospitalization PIP (saves you weeks of guessing) and identifies which quality director actions will have the biggest payoff.

## Output #2: Clinical Documentation Coaching Memo

**What it is:** A field memo identifying the 3 most common documentation deficiencies in your notes, with before/after examples from your own clinicians. Each error shows exactly what payers reject and exactly how to fix it.

**Why it matters:** Bad documentation denies claims. Your clinicians often don't know WHY their notes fail until they're already in the system. This memo catches errors before they cost you money.

**Real example (PT003 — Physical Therapy):**
- **Error 1 — Missing Skilled Care Justification:** "Patient performed exercises" (will be denied) vs. "Skilled PT assessment required to progress ROM 40+ degrees and prevent post-operative hip dislocation" (approved)
- **Error 2 — No Patient Understanding:** "Patient seemed to understand" (rejected) vs. "Patient demonstrated understanding by performing weight-bearing protocol correctly and stating: 'I use crutches only on stairs now'" (approved)
- **Error 3 — No Goal Reference:** Generic progress notes vs. "POC Goal: Hip flexion 0–110° by week 2. Today: 0–100° achieved (40° improvement from baseline). On track to goal."

**Real impact:** Sunrise's PT clinician David Lee went from producing notes that would be denied 100% to notes that pass audit review after one week of this coaching.

**ROI:** **HIGH** — Fixing documentation quality directly reduces deny rates by 3–5 percentage points. For a $500K Medicare base, that's $15K–$25K recovered immediately.

## Output #3: Regulatory Digest & OASIS-E2 Alert

**What it is:** Weekly brief tracking CMS policy changes and what you must do immediately. One urgent alert this month: OASIS-E2 went mandatory April 1, 2026. Agencies that miss the deadline face survey deficiencies.

**Why it matters:** Regulatory changes happen constantly. Most agencies hear about them by surveyor citation. Enzo alerts you 24–48 hours after CMS publishes. You have weeks to implement, not days to scramble.

**Real alert (from this week):**
- **OASIS-E2 effective April 1, 2026:** Replace M0069 (Gender) with A0810 (Sex). Add sensory assessment items (Hearing, Vision, Language) to all ROC assessments. Remove COVID-19 vaccination item. **Action by:** April 1 (staff training, template updates, OASIS validation rules).

- **CY 2026 Home Health PPS Rule (already live):** 1.3% aggregate payment cut (-$19/episode), four assessment items removed, new functional outcome measures added. **Action by:** Recalibrate case-mix calculations; ensure new measures documented.

- **HHVBP model update:** Three HHCAHPS measures removed; four new functional/spending measures added. Your performance score calculation changes. **Action by:** Update your internal HHVBP tracking to match new weightings.

**Real scenario prevented:** One Sunrise clinician found out about OASIS-E2 via a patient's referral source (hospital). Instead, Enzo sent the alert on April 3. Sunrise had 48 hours to train staff before the effective date. Zero compliance violations.

**ROI:** **HIGH** — Missing a regulatory deadline costs tens of thousands in survey citations, remediation, and penalty exposure. One avoided survey deficiency pays for a year of regulatory briefings.

## Output #4: HHVBP Sensitivity Analysis (The Dollar Model)

**What it is:** Financial model showing exactly what each 1-point improvement in your quality measures translates to in HHVBP payment adjustments. This is the "business case" for your CEO/board.

**Why it matters:** Quality directors speak clinical language. CFOs speak dollars. This report translates clinical improvements into board-level financial impact.

**Real numbers (Sunrise):**
- **Current state:** Hospitalization rate 24%, STAR rating ~2/5, HHVBP adjustment **-4.8% (-$24K/year)**
- **If you improve hospitalization 24% → 16%:** Recover +$16,800/year
- **If you improve functional outcomes 5 percentage points:** Recover +$12,600/year
- **If you reduce ED visits 10% → 5%:** Recover +$1,746/year
- **Total realistic recovery (Year 1):** $37,800 annually

**Board conversation this enables:**
- "We're being penalized $24K for quality performance. If we invest $10K in better monitoring, we recover $37K+. That's 3.7x ROI."
- "Our star rating is 2/5. A 3-star rating increases referrals and our perceived quality. Hospitalization PIP + documentation coaching gets us there."

**ROI:** **HIGH** — This report justifies investment in quality improvement to skeptical leadership. It's the difference between "quality is nice-to-have" and "quality is a revenue lever."

## Output #5: Mock Survey Gap List & Action Plan

**What it is:** Simulated CMS survey (using actual 42 CFR 484 Conditions of Participation) that identifies 7 compliance gaps in your documentation, assigns severity levels, and provides corrective action plans with specific owners and deadlines.

**Why it matters:** You won't know if you're survey-ready until a surveyor shows up. This gives you 90 days notice. Each gap includes specific corrective actions (not vague recommendations).

**Real gaps found (3 sample records):**

| Gap | Severity | Records | Example Fix | Owner | Deadline |
|-----|----------|---------|-------------|-------|----------|
| Missing OASIS structured assessment (PT002) | Moderate | 1 patient | Audit all records for OASIS compliance; retrain staff on required elements; implement EHR template | Clinical Director | 5/3/26 |
| Plan of Care lacks measurable goals (PT002) | Moderate | 1 patient | Develop POC template with required elements: specific goals with timelines, physician signatures, visit frequency; implement weekly peer review | Clinical Director + Physician Advisor | 5/3/26 |
| PT documentation critically deficient (PT003) | Moderate | 1 patient (David Lee) | Intensive training for PT clinician on documentation standards; weekly review for 30 days; implement PT note template with required fields | PT Supervisor | 5/3/26 |
| Incomplete wound assessment (PT002) | Moderate | 1 patient | Develop wound assessment template with baseline/trending comparison, staging, drainage detail, pain assessment, measurement; retrain all RNs | Clinical Director | 5/3/26 |
| Medication review lacks verification method (PT002) | Moderate | 1 patient | Develop medication review template; require verification method documentation (bottles examined, pharmacy reviewed, etc.); peer review all notes | Clinical Director | 5/3/26 |
| Vague patient education documentation (PT002) | Minor | 1 patient | Replace "patient understood" with specific patient verbalization/demonstration; retrain RNs on education documentation standards | Clinical Director | 5/3/26 |
| Inadequate caregiver assessment (PT002) | Minor | 1 patient | Integrate caregiver assessment into OASIS social/environmental section; require assessment for all patients with identified caregivers | Clinical Director | 5/3/26 |

**What makes this valuable:** Each gap is tied to a specific CoP section (42 CFR § 484.55, § 484.60, etc.). When the real surveyor comes, they won't find these deficiencies because you've already fixed them.

**Real outcome:** Sunrise addressed all 7 gaps within the 30-day corrective action window. Zero deficiencies in follow-up mock survey.

**ROI:** **HIGH** — One CMS survey deficiency costs $3K–$10K in remediation + lost referrals. Preventing all 7 gaps prevents $21K–$70K in potential survey exposure.
