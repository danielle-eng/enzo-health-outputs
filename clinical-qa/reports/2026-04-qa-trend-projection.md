## Risk Mitigation Strategies

### Risk 1: PT Clinicians Resist Coaching or Don't Apply Changes
**Likelihood:** Moderate (20-25%)
**Impact:** PT discipline improvement stalls at 30-40% pass rate

**Mitigation:**
- Frame coaching as professional development, not punishment
- Clearly explain regulatory/compliance risks of current documentation
- Provide coaching in supportive format (collaborative vs. directive)
- If resistance continues after Week 2, escalate to program director for performance management
- Consider mandatory continuing education requirement for PT clinicians

### Risk 2: RN Clinicians (Torres, Martinez) Don't Apply Coaching to New Notes
**Likelihood:** Low-Moderate (15-20%)
**Impact:** Improvement in rewritten notes but regression in new notes

**Mitigation:**
- After rewrite, conduct follow-up meeting confirming understanding
- Review first new note from each clinician and provide immediate feedback
- Pair Torres/Martinez with top-performing RN (Chen, Johnson, Hayes) for weekly peer review
- Set clear expectation that coaching is not one-time event but ongoing process

### Risk 3: Documentation Checklist Creates Burden or Resistance
**Likelihood:** Low (10-15%)
**Impact:** Clinicians feel micromanaged; compliance decreases

**Mitigation:**
- Frame checklist as tool to ensure consistency and reduce audit risk (not to punish)
- Start with pilot (first 2 weeks) before making mandatory
- Gather feedback from clinicians on checklist usability
- Adjust checklist based on feedback (e.g., simplify, integrate into EMR)
- Celebrate compliance improvements publicly

### Risk 4: PT Supervisor Doesn't Support Investigations or Removes Barriers
**Likelihood:** Low (10%)
**Impact:** PT discipline issues remain unresolved; improvement stalls

**Mitigation:**
- Schedule root cause analysis meeting early (week of April 8)
- Present data-driven evidence of PT documentation gaps
- Engage PT supervisor in problem-solving (don't tell, ask)
- Offer concrete solutions (e.g., EMR template changes, time allocation, training)
- Set clear timeline for corrective action

### Risk 5: Volume of New Notes Increases Beyond QA Capacity
**Likelihood:** Moderate (25-30%)
**Impact:** QA audit backlogs; coaching impact not assessed in real-time

**Mitigation:**
- Prioritize PT and lower-performing RN notes in first 2 weeks of audit
- After coaching is applied, audit all notes weekly
- If volume exceeds capacity, consider audit rotation (audit 80% of notes weekly vs. 100%)
- Escalate backlog risk to program director early
