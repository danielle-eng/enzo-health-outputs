#### **Top 2 Coaching Points for Lower-Performing RNs (Torres, Martinez)**

**COACHING 1: Structured Patient Education Documentation with Specific Quotes**
*Current Practice (Torres, PT002):*
"Educated patient on importance of using inhalers regularly and on proper inhaler technique. Patient performed return demonstration of inhaler use and appeared to understand instructions."

*Coached Practice (Exemplar from Chen, PT001):*
"**Topic 1: Fluid Restriction (1500 mL daily)**

*Implementation Step:* For every education topic, document:
1. Who received education (patient? caregiver? both?)
2. What specific content was covered (don't just say "educated on CHF"; name the specific topics)
3. What the patient/caregiver SAID to demonstrate understanding (specific quote in quotation marks)
4. What barriers or concerns were addressed

*Before/After Example:*
- **Before:** "Patient instructed to monitor blood glucose daily. Patient understood instructions."
- **After:** "Patient education on daily blood glucose monitoring delivered to patient and daughter. Reviewed proper fingerstick technique, timing of checks (fasting and pre-meal), recognition of hypoglycemia symptoms (shakiness, sweating, confusion). Patient demonstrated proper technique and stated 'I'll check my blood sugar before breakfast and lunch like I do now.' Daughter verbalized understanding of need to keep glucose log and report patterns to nurse."

**COACHING 2: Explicit Skilled Care Justification Specific to Patient's Clinical Complexity**
*Current Practice (Martinez, PT009):*
"Skilled nursing required for management of CHF including medication monitoring and patient education."

*Coached Practice (Exemplar from Hayes, PT007):*
"Skilled nursing required for comprehensive respiratory assessment and monitoring given acute COPD exacerbation status and recent hospitalization. Patient requires skilled assessment for early detection of acute decompensation, oxygen therapy management, medication administration and assessment of effectiveness, patient/caregiver education on respiratory self-management, and coordination with physician for escalation of care if status worsens. Risk of acute exacerbation requiring emergency intervention is high."

*Implementation Step:* For every note, complete this framework:
1. State the medical condition with acute status (if applicable): "CHF with acute exacerbation" vs. "stable CHF"
2. Name the specific complex medications that justify skilled nursing: "diuretic dosing adjustment based on response, ACE inhibitor and beta-blocker effectiveness assessment" vs. generic "medication monitoring"
3. Explain the specific clinical monitoring needed: "daily weight trend, edema status, dyspnea assessment" vs. generic "monitoring"
4. Identify the specific education needed: "fluid/sodium restriction, daily weight technique, decompensation warning signs" vs. generic "patient education"
5. State the risk mitigated by skilled nursing: "prevention of acute exacerbation" vs. generic "management"

*Before/After Example:*
- **Before:** "Skilled nursing required for wound care management and patient education."
- **After:** "Skilled nursing required for post-ulcer foot wound assessment and care in 71-year-old Type 2 diabetic patient at high risk for delayed healing and infection complications. Requires skilled assessment of wound healing trajectory, early detection of infection signs (erythema, drainage change, odor), sterile dressing changes per plan of care, diabetes management assessment (medication adherence, glucose control impact on healing), foot inspection and education to prevent new wound formation, and monitoring for non-healing wound requiring physician escalation."

### PT Documentation Crisis: Root Cause Analysis

All three PT notes fail to meet CMS Conditions of Participation for skilled rehabilitation documentation. This is not individual clinician performance variation; this is a systemic documentation gap:

**Critical Issue 1: NO BASELINE ASSESSMENT AT CARE INITIATION**
- PT003 (3 weeks post-op): No documentation of ROM at SOC. Expected ROM for 3 weeks post-hip replacement: 0-90+ degrees. Current ROM unknown. No baseline = no progress measurement possible.
- PT006 (6 weeks post-op): No documentation of ROM at SOC. Expected ROM for 6 weeks post-knee replacement: 0-90-100+ degrees. Current ROM unknown. No baseline = no progress measurement possible.
- PT010 (8 weeks into episode): No baseline ambulation distance documented from SOC (8 weeks ago). Current ambulation 60 feet. Is this progress? Decline? Plateau? Unknown without baseline.

**Root Cause:** PT clinicians are not documenting baseline assessment data. This may indicate:
1. Training gap: PTs do not understand that baseline is required
2. EMR issue: PT assessment form does not have fields for baseline data
3. Time constraint: PTs do not have time to document comprehensive assessment
4. Competency gap: PTs do not know how to measure ROM (in degrees), strength (0-5 scale), or functional capacity

**Critical Issue 2: NO SKILLED CARE JUSTIFICATION**
- PT003: No explanation of why PT is required for hip replacement
- PT006: No explanation of why PT is required for knee replacement at 6 weeks
- PT010: Generic statement with no explanation of why patient cannot be discharged to home exercise program

**Root Cause:** PT clinicians may not understand the level of detail required to establish medical necessity. Generic statements ("exercises performed," "patient tolerating therapy") do not justify skilled services under CMS standards.

**Critical Issue 3: NO OBJECTIVE PROGRESS MEASUREMENT**
- PT003: Statement "mobility improved" with zero supporting data
- PT006: Statement "patient tolerating therapy" with zero supporting data
- PT010: No comparison to prior visit or baseline

**Root Cause:** PTs are documenting subjective impressions without objective measurements. CMS requires measurable progress (ROM in degrees, distance in feet, strength grades) to justify continued skilled services.

**Critical Issue 4: NO PLAN OF CARE GOAL REFERENCE**
- PT003: No goals named; statement "continue current plan"
- PT006: No goals named; no timeline for discharge
- PT010: Generic goal ("improved functional mobility") without specific targets

**Root Cause:** PTs may not be receiving POC goals from supervisors, or may not understand that goals must be referenced in every visit note.

### PT Coaching Topic 1: Baseline Assessment and Objective Progress Measurement

**Current Practice (PT003, Lee):**
"Exercises performed. Patient tolerating therapy well. Mobility improved. Continue with current plan."

**Coached Practice (Model for PT Post-Op Notes):**
"**ROM Assessment:** Hip flexion 0-90 degrees (baseline at SOC 3/15/26 was 0-45 degrees), improved 45 degrees over 3 weeks. Hip abduction 0-30 degrees. Hip internal rotation limited by precautions (intentionally limited to prevent dislocation).

**Strength Assessment:** Hip flexors 3/5 (baseline 2/5, improved from prior week), adequate for supported transfers. Hip abductors 3/5 (critical for gait stability), improved from 2+/5 at SOC. Hip extensors 3/5.

**Functional Assessment:** Ambulation with front-wheeled walker, weight-bearing as tolerated right hip per MD protocol. Patient ambulates 60 feet with walker; prior visit ambulated 45 feet (15-foot improvement over 1 week). Patient ambulation progressing appropriately. Gait pattern antalgic but improving; patient demonstrating decreased reliance on walker support. Transfer: Sit-to-stand with walker support, contact guard assist; prior visit required standby assist (improved to contact guard). Patient demonstrating appropriate post-operative recovery.

**Pain Assessment:** Numeric pain scale 4/10 in left hip incision area, down from 6/10 at prior visit (improvement in pain trajectory).

**Post-Exercise Vital Signs:** Pre-exercise BP 135/78, HR 78, RR 16. Post-exercise BP 138/80, HR 82, RR 18 (appropriate response to therapy). No dizziness or chest discomfort. Patient safely tolerating progression."

*Implementation:* Always include baseline (at SOC or previous visit) and current measurement in same sentence. Example: "Hip ROM baseline 0-45 degrees (SOC), now 0-90 degrees (week 3)" — reader immediately understands 45-degree improvement.

### PT Coaching Topic 2: Skilled Care Justification Specific to Post-Operative Stage

**Current Practice (PT006, Phillips):**
"Skilled PT required for deconditioning management and functional mobility training in patient with chronic COPD and recent acute exacerbation."
[Note: This was for knee replacement, not COPD — indication of copy-paste error or misunderstanding]

**Coached Practice (PT Post-Op Model):**
"**Skilled physical therapy is required for post-operative rehabilitation following total right knee arthroplasty (surgery 2/18/26, 6 weeks post-operative).** Patient requires skilled assessment to:
- Restore knee range of motion (current ROM 0-85 degrees; expected 0-100+ degrees at 6 weeks; ROM still below expected trajectory)
- Restore lower extremity strength (current quad strength 3/5; needed for functional ambulation and stair negotiation)
- Optimize gait mechanics and reduce antalgic gait pattern (patient protecting right knee, limiting normal weight distribution)
- Assess and reduce fall risk (strength deficits and proprioceptive losses post-op increase fall risk)
- Prevent post-operative complications (decreased ROM leads to stiffness; inadequate strength leads to falls; poor gait mechanics lead to contralateral joint stress)

**Therapeutic exercises must be graded, monitored, and modified by licensed PT** to ensure safety and effectiveness. Patient demonstrates appropriate post-operative recovery trajectory but remains below expected ROM/strength at 6 weeks; continued skilled intervention indicated to optimize functional outcomes and achieve independence with mobility and stairs."

*Key Elements:*
1. Name the procedure and timeline (how many weeks post-op)
2. List specific skilled services needed
3. Explain why those services require licensed PT (not aide or patient alone)
4. Identify baseline vs. expected status (shows ROM is below expected, justifying continued skilled services)
5. Identify complications prevented by skilled therapy
