## Executive Summary

This week's audit of 10 home health visit notes reveals significant variability in documentation quality and ADR-readiness. Overall pass rate is 50% (5 of 10 notes scoring ≥7/10). Immediate targeted coaching is needed for PT discipline (0/3 passing; average 1.3/10) and continued monitoring of RN discipline variability (3/5 passing; average 5.6/10).

## Top Documentation Deficiencies (All Notes, Ranked by Frequency)

**1. MISSING BASELINE ASSESSMENT OR FUNCTIONAL COMPARISON (60% of failing notes)**
- Affects: PT003, PT006, PT010 (all PT notes)
- Issue: No baseline ROM/strength documented at start of care; no prior visit comparison; cannot demonstrate progress or justify continued skilled services
- Example: PT003 states "mobility improved" without documenting baseline flexion/extension in degrees or prior ROM measurement
- Impact: AUDIT RISK - CRITICAL; auditors cannot assess skilled progression

**2. VAGUE OR MISSING SKILLED CARE JUSTIFICATION (60% of failing/revision notes)**
- Affects: PT002, PT006, PT009, PT010 (3 RN notes, 2 PT notes)
- Issue: Statement of why skilled services required is generic or absent; no explanation of medical necessity
- Example: PT009 states "Skilled nursing required for management of CHF including medication monitoring" without explaining complexity of regimen or risk of decompensation
- Impact: AUDIT RISK - HIGH; payers question whether visit could be managed at clinic or via telehealth

**3. ABSENT OR MINIMAL PATIENT VERBALIZATION OF UNDERSTANDING (50% of failing notes)**
- Affects: PT002, PT003, PT006, PT009, PT010 (fails + PT notes)
- Issue: No specific quotes or statements from patient/caregiver demonstrating comprehension of education topics
- Example: PT002 states "appeared to understand" instead of documenting specific patient statement
- Impact: AUDIT RISK - MODERATE; education efficacy cannot be verified; weak medical necessity argument

**4. NO PLAN OF CARE GOAL REFERENCE OR PROGRESS DOCUMENTATION (50% of failing notes)**
- Affects: PT002, PT003, PT006, PT009, PT010
- Issue: Notes do not reference specific POC goals or document measurable progress toward goals
- Example: PT010 states "continue current plan" without identifying what the plan is or what outcomes are expected
- Impact: AUDIT RISK - HIGH; auditors question medical necessity for continued visits

**5. INSUFFICIENT FUNCTIONAL ASSESSMENT DETAIL / LACK OF OASIS CONSISTENCY (40% of notes)**
- Affects: PT002, PT003, PT006, PT009, PT010
- Issue: Limited assessment of OASIS-relevant domains (dyspnea severity, edema measurement, mobility distance, assistance level, cognitive status)
- Example: PT006 does not document knee ROM, strength grades, gait pattern, or ambulation distance post-op
- Impact: COMPLIANCE RISK - MODERATE; OASIS scoring accuracy compromised

**6. MINIMAL CLINICAL INTERPRETATION; TASK-FOCUSED RATHER THAN ASSESSMENT-FOCUSED (40% of notes)**
- Affects: PT002, PT003, PT006, PT009, PT010
- Issue: Notes document tasks performed but lack clinical reasoning or skilled assessment
- Example: PT003 states "Exercises done" without explaining what exercises, why those specific exercises, and what response patient demonstrated
- Impact: DOCUMENTATION QUALITY - MODERATE; notes do not demonstrate skilled assessment

2. **PT006 (0/10) — Knee Replacement at 6 Weeks Post-Op:** Similarly deficient. No baseline established at 6 weeks post-op. No ROM/strength progression documented. No exercise detail. 3. **PT010 (4/10) — COPD/Deconditioning at 8 Weeks:** Slightly better than PT003/PT006 but still failing. At 8 weeks, patient should show substantial progress or should be discharged; this note does not establish baseline from SOC, making progress assessment impossible. Auditors will question continued skilled therapy vs. discharge to home exercise program.

**Required Immediate Action:** PT discipline requires mandatory documentation retraining. PT notes are not meeting CMS standards. All three PT clinicians need structured coaching on:
- Baseline assessment documentation (ROM in degrees, strength 0-5, functional measures)
- Skilled care justification (specific, not generic)
- Objective progress measurement (prior visit comparison, functional progression)
- POC goal reference with measurable outcomes
