## Patient Education Provided

*Delivered to:* Patient and wife (primary caregiver)
*Content:* Reviewed specific fluid allowance (water, tea, juice), taught patient how to measure fluids at home using household measuring cup, discussed foods that are liquid at room temperature (ice cream, Jell-O) and must be counted. *Barriers Addressed:* Patient initially concerned about thirst; educated that thirst typically resolves after 3-5 days of restriction. Wife offered strategies to use hard candies or ice chips to manage thirst sensation.

**Topic 2: Daily Weights**
*Delivered to:* Patient
*Content:* Reviewed importance of daily weights as early indicator of fluid retention; weight gain of 3+ lbs in 1 day or 5+ lbs in 1 week signals need for immediate provider contact. Instructed on proper technique: same scale, same time of day (morning after void, before breakfast), in light clothing.
*Patient Verbalization of Understanding:* Patient demonstrated correct scale use and stated "If I gain more than 5 pounds in a week or 3 pounds in one day, I call the doctor right away."

**Topic 3: Signs/Symptoms of Acute Decompensation**
*Delivered to:* Patient and wife
*Content:* Reviewed red flags: increased shortness of breath at rest or with minimal activity, orthopnea, PND, sudden weight gain, chest pain, palpitations, confusion, or severe swelling. Discussed when to contact provider vs. call 911 (chest pain, severe dyspnea = 911).
*Patient Verbalization of Understanding:* Wife demonstrated understanding by correctly identifying two symptoms that would warrant 911 call.
