### ⚠️ Deficiencies Found

**Deficiency 1 — Complete Absence of Skilled Care Justification**
*Finding:* Note contains no statement explaining why physical therapy is medically necessary for this 79-year-old post-hip-replacement patient. No description of assessment findings, no rehabilitation goals, no clinical rationale for continuing PT vs. discharging to home exercise program.
*Standard:* CMS CoP 484.60 requires that each visit note document the specific skilled services provided and medical necessity for those services. For PT post-operatively, this requires documentation of: range of motion assessment, strength testing, functional mobility testing (transfers, ambulation, stairs), balance assessment, and specific therapeutic goals driving the PT plan.
*Risk:* AUDIT RISK - CRITICAL. This note has ZERO defensibility. Payers will deny this visit outright as not meeting skilled rehabilitation criteria. Medicare will not cover PT based on generic statements like "exercises performed."
*Required Correction:* MANDATORY. Add comprehensive skilled justification: "Physical therapy is required for post-operative rehabilitation following total hip arthroplasty (surgery date ~3/15/26) to restore functional mobility, strength, and independence. Patient requires skilled assessment of range of motion (ROM), hip precautions compliance, gait mechanics, and fall risk. Therapeutic exercises must be graded, monitored, and modified by licensed PT to ensure safety and prevent complications (dislocation, deep vein thrombosis, falls)."

**Deficiency 2 — No Functional Assessment or Baseline Documentation**
*Finding:* Note states "Exercises performed," "Patient tolerating therapy well," and "Mobility improved," but contains zero objective assessment data: no ROM measurements, no strength grades, no gait assessment, no balance testing, no functional baseline documented on prior visit to support claim of "improvement."
*Standard:* Skilled PT documentation requires specific measurements and observations: hip ROM (flexion, abduction, internal/external rotation expressed in degrees), strength grading (0-5 scale), gait pattern (antalgic? assistive device use?), transfers (independent? requires contact guard?), stairs (if applicable), balance testing, pain assessment, and functional mobility goals.
*Risk:* AUDIT RISK - CRITICAL. Without baseline or comparison data, auditor cannot determine if this visit provided skilled rehabilitation or was merely a "visit" with no measurable outcome. Denial is virtually certain.
*Required Correction:* MANDATORY. Document: "ROM Assessment: Hip flexion 0-90 degrees (baseline 0-45 degrees at SOC), improved 45 degrees from week 1. Hip abduction 0-30 degrees. Hip internal rotation limited by precautions. Strength: Hip flexors 3/5 (improved from 2/5 at SOC). Hip abductors 3/5. Gait: Ambulating with front-wheeled walker, weight-bearing as tolerated (WBAT) right lower extremity per MD protocol. Antalgic gait pattern improving; patient decreasing reliance on walker (now uses 30 feet with walker, previously 15 feet). Balance: Able to stand 30 seconds with walker support; improving from 15 seconds at SOC. Transfer: Sit-to-stand with walker, contact guard assist (goal: modified independence). Stairs: Not yet attempted per precautions."

**Deficiency 3 — No Documentation of Exercises or Specific Interventions**
*Finding:* Note states "Exercises done" and "Treatment went okay," but does not specify what exercises were performed, how many repetitions, what resistance, or patient tolerance to each intervention.
*Standard:* Skilled PT requires documentation of specific interventions: "Patient performed hip flexion and extension exercises x 15 reps in supine; performed hip abduction lying supine with pillow between knees to enforce precautions x 10 reps; standing hip flexion with walker support x 12 reps; gait training over 50 feet with cues for heel strike and decreased weight on left. All exercises tolerated without increased pain or edema."
*Risk:* DOCUMENTATION QUALITY - CRITICAL. Medical record does not reflect what PT actually did; makes treatment non-reproducible and creates liability if patient has adverse outcome.
*Required Correction:* MANDATORY. Document each exercise: sets, reps, patient tolerance, resistance level, cues needed, pain response, and any modifications made during visit.

**Deficiency 4 — No Patient Response or Understanding Documentation**
*Finding:* Note states "No pain reported today" but contains no documentation of patient comprehension of home exercise program, barriers to exercise, precaution understanding (critical for hip replacement), or patient goals.
*Standard:* Skilled PT requires documentation of patient education regarding home exercise program: specific exercises to perform daily, frequency/duration, precautions (hip flexion >90 degrees, hip adduction, internal rotation to avoid dislocation), when to contact PT/MD (increased pain, swelling, instability), and patient/caregiver verbalization of understanding.
*Risk:* COMPLIANCE/SAFETY RISK. If patient dislocates hip and was never explicitly educated on precautions, liability ensues. Auditor also looks for evidence that patient is engaged in rehabilitation.
*Required Correction:* MANDATORY. Add: "Patient education provided on hip precautions: no hip flexion beyond 90 degrees, no adduction across midline, no internal rotation. Patient verbalized understanding by correctly demonstrating modified sit-to-stand avoiding hip flexion >90 degrees. Patient instructed to perform home exercises (hip flexion, abduction, standing hip flexion) 2x daily, 10-15 reps each. Wife present and confirmed understanding of precautions and exercise regimen. Patient verbalizes pain level 4/10 in left hip, acceptable post-operatively. Wife instructed to contact PT if pain increases significantly or if patient reports hip 'clicking' or instability."

**Deficiency 5 — No OASIS-Consistent Functional Status**
*Finding:* Note contains no reference to OASIS domains or functional assessment relevant to rehabilitation: no ambulation distance documented, no assistive device level specified, no stair negotiation assessed, no transfer independence assessed, no cognitive/safety awareness documented.
*Standard:* PT notes should document functional mobility status using clinically meaningful measures that align with OASIS scoring items (ambulation, transfers, stairs). OASIS requires specific functional capacity measurement (e.g., "ambulates 50 feet with walker, requires supervision," not "mobility improved").
*Risk:* COMPLIANCE RISK - MODERATE. OASIS scoring may be inaccurate if PT notes do not provide detailed functional information.
*Required Correction:* Document OASIS-relevant function: "Ambulation: Patient now ambulates 50 feet with front-wheeled walker, WBAT right lower extremity; gait antalgic but improving. Transfers: Patient requires contact guard assist for sit-to-stand from standard height chair; working toward modified independence. Stairs: Not yet attempted; patient not safe for stairs at this time due to balance deficit (goal: negotiate stairs with rail and supervision by week 4 of PT). Locomotion: Functional ambulation limited to first floor of home; currently unable to manage stairs independently."

**Deficiency 6 — No Plan of Care Goals or Progress Documentation**
*Finding:* Note does not reference POC goals, does not state functional outcome targets, and does not document progress toward any measurable objective. Statement "Continue with current plan" provides no detail on what the plan is or what outcomes are expected.
*Standard:* PT documentation must reference specific functional goals from POC (e.g., "Goal: Patient will ambulate 100 feet with walker and supervision within 3 weeks") and document progress toward those goals with objective measurements.
*Risk:* AUDIT RISK - HIGH. Without goal documentation, auditor cannot determine medical necessity for continued PT. Goals drive skilled rehabilitation; without them, visits appear custodial.
*Required Correction:* MANDATORY. Add POC Progress section: "POC Goal 1: Patient will ambulate 100 feet with walker and supervision without pain >5/10 within 3 weeks of SOC. Progress: Currently ambulates 50 feet with walker; achieved 45-foot improvement from baseline. On track for goal. POC Goal 2: Patient will demonstrate independence with home exercise program and hip precautions understanding. Progress: Patient demonstrated correct precaution technique today; wife educated; continuing reinforcement at next visit. POC Goal 3: Patient will achieve modified independence with sit-to-stand transfers within 2 weeks. Progress: Currently at contact guard level; decreasing assistance appropriately. Target independence by 4/10/26. Continue current plan with focus on strengthening hip extensors and flexors."

**Deficiency 7 — No Homebound Status Documentation**
*Finding:* Note does not address homebound status; no statement of patient's functional limitations or inability to leave residence.
*Standard:* While homebound status may be established at SOC, each note should document functional limitations supporting continued homebound status and home-based PT (e.g., "Patient unable to ambulate to vehicle without significant assistance; 6 weeks post-op, currently requiring walker, contact guard for transfers, pain limiting mobility").
*Risk:* COMPLIANCE RISK - LOW TO MODERATE. Lack of homebound documentation may prompt eligibility questions at higher service frequencies.
*Required Correction:* Add: "Homebound Status: Patient unable to leave residence without considerable assistance due to post-operative mobility limitations, pain, and fall risk. Patient ambulates only with walker and supervision; unable to manage stairs or negotiate uneven terrain. Home-based PT appropriate and necessary at this time."

**Deficiency 8 — Inadequate Vital Signs and Clinical Assessment**
*Finding:* No vital signs documented. No pain assessment beyond "No pain reported." No edema assessment (critical post-operatively to rule out DVT). No surgical site assessment. No clinician assessment of patient's post-operative recovery trajectory.
*Standard:* PT post-op notes should include basic vital signs (to detect infection or cardiovascular complications), pain assessment using numeric scale, edema check on surgical limb, surgical site inspection for signs of infection (if visible), and clinical judgment about post-operative progress.
*Risk:* SAFETY/DOCUMENTATION RISK. Without basic post-operative monitoring documentation, potential complications (infection, DVT, cardiac issues) could be missed, creating patient safety and liability issues.
*Required Correction:* Document: "Vital Signs: BP 135/78, HR 78, RR 16, SpO2 98% RA. Pain: 4/10 in left hip incision area, decreased from 6/10 at prior visit. Edema: Mild left ankle edema (1+), improving; no calf tenderness or warmth (DVT screening negative). Surgical site: Small sterile dressing in place; no drainage, erythema, or drainage odor noted. Post-operative progress: Patient recovering appropriately; pain and swelling trending favorably. No signs of infection or DVT."

## Recommended Revisions

**This note requires COMPLETE REWRITE.** The current version contains virtually no substantive clinical information and will be immediately denied on ADR review.

1. **ADD SKILLED CARE JUSTIFICATION** — Explicitly state why PT is medically necessary post-hip replacement (ROM restoration, strength recovery, gait training, fall prevention, dislocation precautions).

2. **DOCUMENT SPECIFIC ASSESSMENT FINDINGS** — ROM measurements (in degrees), strength grades (0-5 scale), gait pattern, transfer ability, ambulation distance, balance assessment, pain level (numeric scale).

3. **LIST SPECIFIC EXERCISES PERFORMED** — Name each exercise, repetitions, sets, resistance, patient tolerance, modifications made.

4. **DOCUMENT PATIENT/CAREGIVER EDUCATION** — Specific precautions taught (hip flexion/adduction/internal rotation limits), home exercise program prescribed, patient/wife verbalization of understanding.

5. **ADD HOMEBOUND STATEMENT** — Functional limitations preventing leaving residence; rationale for home-based PT.

6. **REFERENCE POC GOALS** — Name specific functional goals (ROM targets, strength targets, ambulation distance, transfer independence); document progress toward each goal with objective measurements.

7. **INCLUDE BASIC VITAL SIGNS** — Temperature, BP, HR, RR, SpO2; basic post-operative safety screening.

8. **ADD OASIS-CONSISTENT FUNCTIONAL DETAIL** — Specific ambulation distance, assistive device, supervision level; transfer level of assistance; stair negotiation status.

9. **PROVIDE CLINICAL ASSESSMENT** — Summary statement on post-operative recovery trajectory, pain and edema trends, safety status, plan for next visit.
