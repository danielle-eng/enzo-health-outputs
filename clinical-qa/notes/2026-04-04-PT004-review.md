## Detailed Findings

### ✅ Strengths

- **Explicit Skilled Care Justification:** Note clearly states why OT intervention is required: "ADL retraining following acute stroke with residual left-sided weakness and motor planning deficits." Specific cognitive/perceptual deficits identified (left-sided neglect). This meets CMS standard for establishing medical necessity for skilled OT.

- **Comprehensive Cognitive/Perceptual Assessment:** Documentation includes specific assessment of left-sided neglect with behavioral observation: "does not spontaneously scan left side of visual field." Orientation deficits documented (not oriented to current month). Problem-solving deficits noted. This level of cognitive detail supports skilled OT justification.

- **ADL Training Documented with Functional Context:** Three ADL areas systematically assessed: bathing, dressing, grooming. For each, current functional level documented with specific assistance requirements:
  - Bathing: "moderate assistance" with specific functional barriers identified (standing balance impairment)
  - Dressing: Upper/lower body differentiated; specific deficits linked to impairment (left arm weakness, left-sided neglect)
  - Grooming: Motor vs. cognitive deficits separated (motor ability intact, but neglect causes functional problem)

- **Adaptive Strategies and Patient Demonstration:** Note documents not just what was taught, but patient response. Example: "Patient demonstrated understanding by verbally confirming techniques and attempting left arm reach during assessment." For grooming, "Patient demonstrated use of cueing during visit." This shows active patient engagement, not passive instruction.

- **Caregiver Training and Verbalization:** Wife education explicitly documented. Wife verbalization of understanding documented: "Wife verbalized understanding of need to position clothing to prompt patient's attention to left side." This demonstrates the wife is engaged and capable of implementing therapy goals, which is critical given patient's cognitive/perceptual deficits.

- **Specific Safety Concerns Identified:** Note identifies three concrete safety concerns: bathroom grab bars, patient safety awareness (fall risk from neglect), and caregiver strain. Recommendations are specific and actionable (grab bar installation, continued OT for safety training).

- **OASIS-Consistent Functional Detail:** Ambulation documented with specific distance and assistance level: "Patient ambulates 30 feet with quad cane and moderate supervision." Transfers documented: "Sit to stand require contact guard assistance." Stairs addressed specifically: "not assessed due to safety precautions." This level of detail allows for accurate OASIS scoring.

- **Clinical Reasoning Evident:** Note demonstrates skilled assessment, not merely task completion. Recognition of left-sided neglect as a distinct therapeutic challenge; understanding of how to adapt bathing setup to address both physical weakness and cognitive/perceptual deficits; recognition of caregiver strain as a clinical concern.

### ⚠️ Deficiencies Found

**None identified.** This note demonstrates comprehensive occupational therapy documentation meeting all standards for skilled rehabilitation post-stroke. Documentation establishes medical necessity, includes specific functional assessments, documents patient/caregiver understanding, and identifies clear therapeutic targets (ADL independence with adaptive techniques).
