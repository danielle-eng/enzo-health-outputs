### ⚠️ Deficiencies Found

**Deficiency 1 — Absence of Explicit Skilled Care Justification**
*Finding:* Note states "Wound care performed" and documents education on inhalers and wound care, but contains no explicit statement explaining why skilled nursing is medically necessary. There is no sentence such as "Skilled nursing is required for..." or explanation of what clinical expertise justifies the nurse visit.
*Standard:* CMS Conditions of Participation (CoP 484.60) requires documentation of the specific skilled services being provided and the medical necessity for those services. Each note must establish why the intervention requires a licensed nurse (vs. aide or caregiver).
*Risk:* AUDIT RISK - HIGH. Payers may deny visit as custodial care (non-skilled wound care or education) if skilled necessity is not explicitly documented. ADR reviewers specifically look for this statement.
*Required Correction:* Add a dedicated "Skilled Care Justification" section stating: "Skilled nursing is required for assessment of wound healing progress, detection of signs of infection (erythema, drainage changes, odor), sterile dressing changes per POC, and assessment of COPD status including medication response. This 68-year-old patient with COPD and post-ulcer wound requires skilled assessment of pulmonary status and wound healing trajectory to prevent complications."

---

**Deficiency 2 — Vague Patient Response Documentation / No Verbalization of Understanding**
*Finding:* Education section notes patient "appeared to understand instructions" on inhaler technique and was "encouraged" to use inhalers, but contains no specific statement of what patient said, demonstrated, or confirmed. Similarly, wound care education states patient was "instructed" but does not document patient's comprehension or agreement.
*Standard:* CMS guidance and industry standards require that clinician document what the patient SAID or DID to demonstrate understanding. Examples: "Patient stated...", "Patient demonstrated...", "Patient verbalized understanding that...", or "Wife confirmed understanding by saying..."
*Risk:* COMPLIANCE/AUDIT RISK - MODERATE. Lack of patient verbalization weakens the medical necessity argument for skilled education, particularly if care is challenged. Documentation does not prove patient actually understood or will comply.
*Required Correction:* Revise education section to include: "Patient performed return demonstration of inhaler use, correctly assembling metered-dose inhaler and executing proper technique (shaking before use, slow inhalation, 10-second breath hold). Patient stated 'I'll use this twice a day, morning and evening.'" And for wound care: "Wife verbalized understanding of need to keep wound clean and dry, stating 'I'll make sure she doesn't get it wet.'"

---

**Deficiency 3 — No Plan of Care (POC) Goal Reference or Goal Progress Documentation**
*Finding:* Note does not reference any POC goals or document progress toward specific functional or clinical goals. Wound status is described generically ("healing appropriately") without linking to a specific goal. No functional improvement or baseline comparison documented.
*Standard:* Each visit note must reference at least one relevant POC goal and document progress toward that goal using objective or subjective measures. This demonstrates that the skilled visit is purposeful and evidence-based, not just routine.
*Risk:* AUDIT RISK - MODERATE TO HIGH. Auditors scrutinize notes for evidence that skilled visits are driving toward measurable functional or clinical outcomes. Lack of goal documentation suggests the visit may not be medically necessary.
*Required Correction:* Add a "Plan of Care Progress" section with examples such as: "POC Goal 1: Patient will achieve complete wound healing (0 cm depth) within 6 weeks through skilled wound assessment and dressing management. Progress: Wound remains 1 cm in depth; no increase in drainage or odor noted. Slight erythema around edges consistent with normal healing phase. Wound healing on track. POC Goal 2: Patient will maintain pulmonary function and prevent COPD exacerbation through medication adherence and inhaler technique. Progress: Patient tolerating current inhaler regimen; no acute dyspnea noted today. Continue monitoring."

---

**Deficiency 4 — Incomplete OASIS-Consistent Functional Status Documentation**
*Finding:* Note includes limited functional observations (ambulation, fatigue, ADL assistance) but lacks systematic assessment of OASIS-relevant domains. No specific dyspnea severity assessment (mild vs. moderate vs. severe; present at rest vs. exertion). No edema assessment despite COPD/potential cardiac component. No cognitive status documentation. No mood/depression screening.
*Standard:* OASIS consistency requires that clinician assess and document patient functional status within domains that align with OASIS scoring items. These include: dyspnea (severity, triggers), lower extremity edema, mobility/ambulation, cognition, mood, and ADL dependence.
*Risk:* COMPLIANCE RISK - MODERATE. Incomplete functional assessment may result in OASIS scoring inaccuracies and inconsistent skilled need justification across visits.
*Required Correction:* Add observations: "Dyspnea: Patient reports dyspnea with exertion; denies dyspnea at rest. Able to speak in full sentences. No orthopnea or PND reported. Edema: No lower extremity edema noted today; skin intact, no skin breakdown. Cognitive: Alert and oriented x3; follows commands appropriately. Mood: Patient reports stable mood; wife notes patient is engaged and cooperative."

---

**Deficiency 5 — Lack of Clinical Interpretation in Vital Signs and Wound Assessment**
*Finding:* Vital signs listed with numbers but no clinical interpretation provided (e.g., "RR 20" is documented but not interpreted as elevated for baseline or concerning for COPD status). Similarly, wound erythema is noted but not assessed for severity or change from prior visit.
*Standard:* Skilled nursing notes should demonstrate clinical reasoning by interpreting vital signs and assessments in context of patient diagnosis. This shows the nurse is performing skilled assessment, not merely collecting data.
*Risk:* DOCUMENTATION QUALITY RISK - LOW TO MODERATE. While not a direct audit trigger, weak clinical interpretation reduces the overall persuasiveness of the note and may prompt questions about the level of nursing skill involved.
*Required Correction:* Expand vital signs interpretation: "RR 20 at baseline for COPD patient; slightly elevated but within expected range for patient with chronic obstructive disease; no acute distress noted." And wound assessment: "Erythema around wound edges consistent with normal inflammatory phase of healing; unchanged from prior visit. No purulent drainage or foul odor noted (both red flags for infection). Wound tracking appropriately on expected healing trajectory."

---

**Deficiency 6 — Minimal Medication Review; No Adherence Assessment Evidence**
*Finding:* Note lists four medications and states patient is "taking them regularly" and "fine," but provides no clinical assessment of medication response, side effects, or evidence-based adherence check (e.g., no bottle dates reviewed, no specific timing confirmed, no symptom assessment related to each drug).
*Standard:* Skilled medication management requires documentation of specific adherence-checking actions and assessment of medication effectiveness in relation to patient's clinical goals.
*Risk:* DOCUMENTATION RISK - MODERATE. Without detailed medication review, the medical necessity for skilled nursing oversight is undermined.
*Required Correction:* Revise to: "Albuterol inhaler—patient reports using twice daily; last refill 3/28/26 (5 days ago), consistent with expected usage. Patient denies wheezing at rest or significant dyspnea with current regimen, suggesting adequate bronchodilation. Tiotropium inhaler—patient verbalizes daily use; reports no side effects. Lisinopril—taken daily, no orthostatic dizziness or persistent cough reported; BP adequate on current dose. Metformin—taken as prescribed; patient reports no GI upset or tolerance issues."

## Recommended Revisions

1. **Add Explicit Skilled Care Justification Section** at the beginning of the clinical assessment, clearly stating why this 68-year-old patient with COPD and post-ulcer foot wound requires skilled nursing (assessment of wound healing, detection of infection, skilled dressing technique, COPD management, medication assessment).

2. **Document Specific Patient Verbalization/Demonstration** in the Patient Education section. Include direct quotes or specific statements of what patient/wife said or did to demonstrate understanding (e.g., "Patient correctly assembled inhaler and demonstrated proper technique" or "Wife stated she would keep wound clean and dry").

3. **Add Plan of Care Progress Section** that references specific POC goals for wound healing and COPD management, and documents objective or subjective progress toward each goal (wound measurements, wound characteristics, pulmonary status, medication response).

4. **Expand Functional Status Documentation** to include OASIS-consistent assessments: dyspnea severity and triggers, lower extremity edema status, cognitive status, mood/depression screening, and ADL/IADLs with specific assistance required.

5. **Add Clinical Interpretation to Vital Signs and Wound Assessment**, explaining what each finding means in context of patient's COPD and healing wound (e.g., "RR 20 is baseline for COPD," "slight erythema consistent with normal healing phase").

6. **Strengthen Medication Review** by documenting specific medication adherence checks (refill dates, timing verification) and clinical response to each medication (symptom control, side effects, clinical goals achieved).
