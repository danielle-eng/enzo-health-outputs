### ⚠️ Deficiencies Found

**Deficiency 1 — Absence of Explicit Skilled Care Justification**
*Finding:* Note contains no statement explaining why PT is medically necessary for this 75-year-old post-operative knee replacement patient. No statement of rehabilitation goals, no explanation of why patient requires skilled PT vs. self-directed home exercise program, no clinical rationale for continued PT services.
*Standard:* CMS Conditions of Participation (CoP 484.60) require documentation of specific skilled services and medical necessity. For post-operative PT, this includes: ROM status, strength assessment, gait mechanics assessment, functional mobility goals, and specific therapeutic interventions that require licensed PT skill.
*Risk:* AUDIT RISK - CRITICAL. Without explicit skilled care justification, payers will deny this visit as not meeting rehabilitation criteria. Medicare reviewers look for this statement in every PT note.
*Required Correction:* Add statement: "Skilled physical therapy is required for post-operative rehabilitation following total right knee arthroplasty (surgery date 2/18/26, 6 weeks post-operative) to restore functional mobility, strength, and independence. Patient requires skilled assessment of knee range of motion, strength recovery, gait mechanics, and fall risk. Therapeutic exercises must be graded, monitored, and modified by licensed PT to ensure safety and prevent post-operative complications (DVT, stiffness, falls, gait dysfunction)."

**Deficiency 2 — No Objective Functional Assessment or Baseline Documentation**
*Finding:* Note states "exercises performed," "patient tolerating therapy," and "no pain reported," but contains zero objective measurements:
  - No knee ROM documented (flexion/extension in degrees)
  - No strength grading (0-5 scale for knee flexors/extensors/hip abductors)
  - No gait assessment documented
  - No ambulation distance documented
  - No stair assessment documented
  - No comparison to baseline or prior visit
  - No baseline established at 6 weeks post-op

*Standard:* Skilled PT documentation requires specific measurements and observations for post-operative knee replacement:
  - ROM: knee flexion/extension with specific degree measurements; compare to expected progression at 6 weeks (typically ROM 0-90-100 degrees at this point)
  - Strength: hip and knee muscle grades (0-5 scale) for functional movement
  - Gait: weight-bearing status, assistive device use, gait pattern description
  - Function: ambulation distance, stair negotiation ability, transfer independence
  - Pain: numeric pain scale, pain location, pain with specific activities

*Risk:* AUDIT RISK - CRITICAL. Without baseline or objective measures, auditor cannot determine if this PT visit provided skilled rehabilitation or was custodial care. Complete denial likely.
*Required Correction:* MANDATORY. Document: "ROM Assessment: Knee flexion 0-85 degrees (expected 90-100 at 6 weeks post-op; patient below expected ROM likely due to pain management or swelling). Knee extension 0 degrees (minimal lag, appropriate). Strength: Knee extensors 3/5 (needed for stair negotiation and functional ambulation). Knee flexors 3/5. Hip abductors 3/5 (critical for gait stability). Gait: Ambulating with front-wheeled walker, weight-bearing as tolerated (WBAT) right lower extremity per MD protocol. Antalgic gait pattern evident; patient protecting right knee. Ambulates approximately 60 feet before fatigue. Stair access: Patient able to negotiate stairs with handrail and contact guard assistance; one step at a time pattern (appropriate post-op). Transfer: Sit-to-stand requires upper extremity support (walker use); contact guard assist; goal is modified independence with straight cane by week 8."

**Deficiency 3 — Vague Exercise Documentation; No Specific Intervention Detail**
*Finding:* Note states "Performed exercises including quad sets, straight leg raises, hamstring stretches, and knee flexion/extension exercises," but provides no documentation of:
  - Number of repetitions or sets for each exercise
  - Resistance level (gravity-eliminated, against gravity, with resistance)
  - Patient tolerance to each exercise (pain level, fatigue, ease/difficulty)
  - Modifications made during visit
  - Home exercise program prescribed (frequency, duration, specific exercises)

*Standard:* Skilled PT documentation requires specific detail of therapeutic exercises performed and prescribed:
  - "Patient performed quad sets x 20 reps, bilaterally, 3 sets, with appropriate vastus medialis activation palpated. Right knee quad set demonstrates adequate recruitment for functional strength. No increase in knee swelling noted post-exercise."
  - "Straight leg raises performed supine: right lower extremity x 15 reps, patient able to maintain extended knee position without lag. No increase in pain. Left lower extremity x 15 reps for comparison and symmetry check."
  - "Home exercise program: Quad sets x 20 reps, 3x daily; straight leg raises x 15 reps, 1x daily; hamstring stretches (supine, moderate stretch, 30-second hold) x 3 reps, 1x daily. Wife present and educated on exercise supervision; wife verbalized understanding of precautions and progression triggers."

*Risk:* DOCUMENTATION QUALITY - CRITICAL. Medical record does not reflect what PT actually did; non-reproducible; creates liability if patient has adverse outcome.
*Required Correction:* Document each exercise with sets, reps, resistance, and patient response. Include home exercise program frequency and patient/caregiver understanding.

Patient at fall risk due to strength deficits. Home-based PT appropriate for safety and accessibility."
*Risk:* COMPLIANCE RISK - MODERATE. Lack of homebound documentation may prompt eligibility questions.
*Required Correction:* Add homebound statement documenting functional limitations and rationale for home-based services.

**Deficiency 5 — No Plan of Care Goal Reference or Progress Documentation**
*Finding:* Note does not reference any POC goals. Statement "Will continue with current exercise program" provides no detail on what specific functional outcomes are expected or timeline for achieving them.
*Standard:* Each PT note must reference specific POC goals and document progress with objective measurements. Example: "POC Goal 1: Patient will achieve knee ROM 0-110 degrees within 2 weeks through therapeutic exercises and active ROM training. Progress: Currently 0-85 degrees; 25-degree improvement needed; on track with current plan. POC Goal 2: Patient will ambulate 150 feet with walker and supervision without pain >4/10 within 3 weeks. Progress: Currently ambulates 60 feet; 90-foot improvement needed; continue current therapeutic plan with focus on quad and hip strengthening."
*Risk:* AUDIT RISK - HIGH. Without goal documentation, auditor cannot determine medical necessity for continued PT. Goals drive skilled rehabilitation; without them, visits appear custodial.
*Required Correction:* MANDATORY. Reference specific POC functional goals and document measurable progress toward each goal.

**Deficiency 6 — Minimal Vital Signs Context; No Post-Exercise Assessment**
*Finding:* Vital signs listed (BP, HR, RR documented; pain reported as "no pain"). However, no clinical interpretation provided and no post-exercise vital signs or response assessment documented.
*Standard:* Post-operative PT notes should include: pre-exercise vital signs, post-exercise vital signs (to assess cardiopulmonary response to therapy), pain assessment using numeric scale, edema assessment (critical post-op to screen for DVT), surgical site assessment if visible, and clinical judgment about post-operative recovery trajectory.
*Risk:* SAFETY/DOCUMENTATION RISK. Without post-exercise vital signs monitoring, potential complications (cardiac issues, DVT) could be missed.
*Required Correction:* Document: "Pre-exercise vitals: BP 140/82, HR 72. Post-exercise vitals: BP 142/84, HR 78 (appropriate response to therapy). Pain: 4/10 in right knee, manageable. Edema: 1+ in right knee, unchanged from baseline; no calf tenderness or warmth (DVT screening negative). Surgical site: Incision well-healed; no drainage, erythema, or swelling noted."
