# Referral Source Checklist
## What Enzo Health Needs to Admit Your Patient

**For:** Physicians, Discharge Planners, Hospital Case Managers

This checklist ensures your home health referral gets **fast approval and quick admission**. Missing items cause delays—sometimes up to 2 weeks.

---

## BEFORE You Submit the Referral

- [ ] **Physician's NPI is valid**
  - Use the CMS NPI lookup tool: https://npiregistry.cms.hhs.gov/
  - Common mistake: Wrong digits or old NPI number
  - If invalid: Contact your EMR vendor or medical staff office to correct your provider record

- [ ] **For Medicare Advantage (MA) patients: Prior authorization is requested**
  - Call the health plan **during the patient's last hospital day**—do not wait until after discharge
  - Common plans & turnaround times:
    - Aetna: 24–48 hours
    - UnitedHealth: 48–72 hours
    - Humana: 24–48 hours
    - BlueCross/BlueShield (varies by state): 48–72 hours
  - **Do NOT send referral until auth is obtained**
  - MA patients without auth cannot start home health

- [ ] **Diagnosis is appropriate (ICD-10 coding)**
  - ✓ Use the **underlying disease code**
    - Example: I50.9 (Heart failure) instead of R06.02 (Shortness of breath)
    - Example: E11.65 (Type 2 diabetes with foot ulcer) instead of L89.91 (Pressure ulcer) alone
  - ✗ Do NOT use R-codes (R52.9 "pain unspecified") as primary diagnosis
  - ✗ Do NOT use Z-codes as primary diagnosis unless truly the reason for home care
  - When in doubt, use your hospital's compliance officer or medical coder to verify

## REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION PACKET

Submit ONE complete referral with **ALL** of these items. Incomplete packets cause 1–2 week delays.

### Clinical Documentation

- [ ] **Physician Plan of Care (signed and dated by the ordering physician)**
  - Must show ordered visit frequencies (e.g., "RN 3x/week, PT 2x/week")
  - Must include discharge diagnosis
  - Handwritten signature or electronic signature acceptable

- [ ] **Face-to-Face Encounter Documentation**
  - REQUIRED by Medicare for ALL patients
  - Must be completed by an MD or DO (NP/PA cannot do initial F2F)
  - Documentation must be in the hospital discharge summary or physician notes
  - Examples of acceptable wording:
    - "Patient evaluated on 4/3/26 by Dr. Sarah Johnson in acute care for home health suitability; appropriate for home health services"
    - "Bedside assessment completed, patient homebound due to [reason], appropriate for HH"

- [ ] **Homebound Status (clear documentation in discharge summary)**
  - Why can patient not leave home without considerable/taxing effort?
  - Include AT LEAST one of these:
    - **Physical:** Bedbound, confined to wheelchair, severe dyspnea on exertion, non-weight bearing
    - **Medical:** Wound care needs, IV therapy, catheter care, oxygen therapy, immunosuppression
    - **Safety:** Fall risk, cognitive decline, unsafe if alone, requires assistance for ADLs
  - Vague language ("frail") is not sufficient
  - Examples of strong documentation:
    - "Patient bedbound due to acute CVA; unable to ambulate; requires total assistance for ADLs"
    - "Non-weight bearing status post knee replacement; requires crutches for any mobility"

- [ ] **Past Medical & Surgical History (brief summary acceptable)**
  - Medications list (current and at discharge)
  - Relevant allergies (medication, food, contact)

### Insurance Documentation

- [ ] **Insurance card copy (front & back) or member ID**
  - Verify:
    - Is this Medicare Traditional (Part A) or MA (Advantage)?
    - Is this Medicaid?
    - Is this Private insurance with HH benefit?
  - If unsure which type: Ask patient, or state insurance department can verify

- [ ] **For Medicare Advantage: Proof of Prior Authorization**
  - Print or screenshot of the MA plan approval (or confirmation #)
  - Turnaround: Usually within 24–72 hours of request
  - If auth pending: Referral can be submitted with "auth pending" note, but patient cannot START until auth is received

- [ ] **For Commercial Insurance: Verification of Home Health Benefit**
  - Some commercial plans do NOT cover home health; must verify before referral
  - Best practice: Call plan and ask "Does this plan cover home health services?" and request written confirmation

### Operational Information

- [ ] **Patient address and phone number**
  - Address must be within Enzo Health service area (Central Ohio: Columbus metro + adjacent counties)
  - Verify patient is OK with Enzo Health as provider

- [ ] **Equipment needs explicitly listed (if any)**
  - Examples: Oxygen, IV pump, specialty wound dressings, hospital bed, lift equipment
  - Allows us to coordinate equipment delivery with patient admission

## WHAT KILLS A REFERRAL

**These issues cause automatic decline; avoid them:**

| Issue | Why It's a Problem | How to Fix It |
|-------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Invalid NPI** | Federal requirement; cannot bill without valid NPI | Verify in CMS NPI lookup tool before submitting |
| **No MA auth** | Cannot start MA patient without insurance approval | Call MA plan BEFORE discharge; include auth # in referral |
| **R-code primary diagnosis** | Coding compliance; symptoms don't justify home health | Use underlying disease (e.g., I50.9 instead of R06.02) |
| **No face-to-face** | Medicare requirement | MD/DO must document F2F in discharge summary |
| **Homebound status not documented** | Medicare requirement | Add 1–2 sentences to discharge summary explaining why patient is homebound |
| **No Plan of Care** | Legal requirement; defines what we're authorized to do | Include physician-signed visit orders in referral packet |
| **Out of service area** | Cannot serve; no clinicians available | Check address before referral; contact Enzo if unsure |

## COMMON QUESTIONS

**Q: What if I don't have the face-to-face completed yet?**
A: The F2F must be completed before the referral is sent. If you're referring on the day of discharge, the F2F should be done that morning. Do not send referral without F2F documentation.

**Q: Does an NP or PA count for face-to-face?**
A: No. Only MD or DO can complete the required F2F encounter.

**Q: How long does approval take?**
A: For traditional Medicare: 2–3 business days. For MA patients with auth: 1–2 days. For patients pending MA auth: 3–5 days after auth received.

**Q: Can you see the patient if they're not yet Medicare-eligible?**
A: If they have Medicare Part A (post-acute care): yes. If they have Medicare Advantage: yes (with auth). If they have commercial insurance only: maybe—call to verify home health is covered.

**Q: What if the patient wants a different home health agency?**
A: That's fine; we're one option among many. But if you refer to us, this checklist applies.

**Q: We're a smaller clinic and don't know how to verify MA auth. Can Enzo help?**
A: Ideally no—the discharging facility should verify MA auth before referral. But in urgent cases, call us at (614) 555-0100 and ask the intake team for guidance.
