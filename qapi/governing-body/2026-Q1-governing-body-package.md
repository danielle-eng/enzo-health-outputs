## Areas of Concern Requiring Board Attention

### 1. Acute Care Hospitalization Rate: 24.0% (vs. 14.7% Benchmark) — RED ALERT

**The Issue:** Sunrise Health's hospitalization rate is 9.3 percentage points above the national benchmark, representing 12 of 50 patients hospitalized during their home health episodes. This translates to approximately $50,000-$75,000 in excess acute care costs per quarter.

**Why It Matters:** 
- Hospitalizations indicate failure to prevent acute exacerbations or inadequate early detection/response
- Generates unnecessary costs and disruptive care transitions for vulnerable elderly patients
- Affects patient satisfaction and quality of life
- May signal gaps in care coordination, patient education, or monitoring protocols

**What We're Doing:**
- Formal Performance Improvement Project (PIP) launched April 4, 2026
- Root causes identified: insufficient early warning sign recognition, limited proactive monitoring, gaps in physician communication, and inadequate patient/caregiver education
- Seven targeted interventions underway, including: standardized early warning sign protocols, 24/7 clinical triage line, structured monitoring for high-risk diagnoses (CHF, COPD), enhanced physician communication protocols, and improved patient education materials
- **Interim target:** Reduce to ≤20% by Q2 2026; final target ≤16% by Q3 2026

**Board Action Requested:** Acknowledge the hospitalization rate concern and support implementation of PIP interventions, including potential allocation for remote monitoring devices and staffing adjustments for after-hours triage.

---

### 2. Emergency Department Utilization: 10.0% (vs. 8.2% Benchmark) — RED ALERT

**The Issue:** Five patients (10%) accessed the emergency department during their home health episodes, compared to the 8.2% benchmark. While the gap is smaller than hospitalization, it represents preventable emergency utilization.

**Why It Matters:**
- ED visits often indicate patients/caregivers do not know when to call home health vs. 911
- Increases costs, creates care fragmentation, and disrupts clinical continuity
- Correlates with higher risk of hospitalization and adverse outcomes

**What We're Doing:**
- Included in hospitalization reduction PIP with specific focus on patient/caregiver education ("when to call home health vs. 911")
- 24/7 triage line will provide clinical guidance and prevent unnecessary ED referrals
- Structured protocols for after-hours access reduce reliance on ED

**Board Action Requested:** Same as above; ED reduction will be achieved through hospitalization reduction interventions.

---

### 3. Timely Initiation of Care: 92.0% (vs. 96.3% Benchmark) — YELLOW ALERT

**The Issue:** Four patients (8%) had delayed OASIS SOC (Start of Care) assessments, completed more than 14 days after admission. While the gap appears modest at 4 percentage points, timely initial assessment is foundational to care quality.

**Why It Matters:**
- Delays in SOC compromise initial risk stratification and care planning
- May result in missed identification of early warning signs or contraindications
- Affects regulatory compliance and documentation standards

**What We're Doing:**
- New process PIP targeting same-week SOC completion
- Weekly monitoring report of SOC scheduling and completion status
- Administrative escalation protocols for any OASIS SOC beyond 7 days

**Board Action Requested:** Acknowledge the initiative and support staffing/scheduling adjustments to ensure assessment capacity in the first week post-admission.
